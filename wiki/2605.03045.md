# TCD-Arena: Assessing Robustness of Time Series Causal Discovery Methods Against Assumption Violations (2605.03045)

- **Year**: 2026.05 (ICLR 2026 camera-ready; `\iclrfinalcopy` active in source, i.e. accepted/de-anonymized version)
- **Authors**: Gideon Stein, Niklas Penzel, Tristan Piater, Joachim Denzler — Computer Vision Group Jena, Friedrich Schiller University Jena, Germany
- **Venue**: ICLR 2026
- **Keywords**: time series causal discovery, robustness benchmark, assumption violations, structural causal model, ensembling, hyperparameter sensitivity, synthetic data generation, model misspecification
- **One-liner**: TCD-Arena stepwise-intensifies 33 real-world-inspired assumption violations across ~36M causal-discovery attempts to profile the robustness of 10 time-series CD methods, and shows simple ensembling improves general robustness.

## 精华提炼

### 管线/方法

1. **Base SCM.** All violations perturb a single linear-additive base model with both lagged and instantaneous coefficients: `X_t = B·X_t + A·X_{t-1...t-L} + E` (Eq. base_eq), where non-zero entries of `A`/`B` are edges of the ground-truth causal graph `G`. For nonlinear violations, `f_{i,d,l}` replaces the identity link.
2. **Three target graphs per method/violation**: `G^LWCG` (lagged window causal graph, lag-specific), `G^LSG` (lagged summary graph, collapsed over lags), `G^INST` (instantaneous-only graph). Recovering `G^INST` is structurally harder (resembles i.i.d. CD) than the lagged graphs.
3. **Data regimes.** 16 combinations from `(D,L) ∈ {(5,3),(7,4)}` ("small"/"big"), link probabilities `p_lag ∈ {0.075,0.15}`, `p_inst ∈ {0,0.1}`, series length `T ∈ {250,1000}`.
4. **Violation calibration (3-step recipe, per violation type)**: (a) find a parameter level causing minor degradation of the Cross-Correlation baseline, (b) find the level pushing baseline AUROC to ~0.5 (chance) where the violation type allows it, (c) space 5 discrete levels evenly between those two anchors. This explicitly sacrifices cross-violation comparability to keep each violation's range meaningful.
5. **Sampling scale.** 100 SCM+time-series draws per (violation × severity level × data regime); 8,000 instances per violation, except 3 violations (`V_faith,inst`, `V_conf,inst`, `V_length`) that halve the regime space (4,000 each) because they are only meaningful for one branch of a binary setting (e.g. `p_inst≠0`, or length itself is the violated variable). Total: `30×8000 + 3×4000 = 252,000` SCM/time-series pairs.
6. **Stability gates during generation**: acyclicity check for instantaneous coefficients via `tr(e^B) = D`; VAR stationarity via companion-matrix eigenvalues (`max|λ(F)| < 1`) for linear SCMs; for nonlinear SCMs, an explicit monotonic-divergence test over a rolling window (`T=10`) and a hard magnitude bound (`±25`) — any violation triggers a resample of the whole SCM, not just the noise draw.
7. **Method × HP sweep.** 10 CD methods, 143 total hyperparameter configurations (counts per method: CrossCorr 3, CausalPretraining 2, Varlingam 6, GVAR 6, PCMCI 6, PCMCI+ 12, SVAR-RFCI 6, F-PCMCI 6, Dynotears 48, NTS-NOTears 48) → `252,000 × 143 = 36,036,000` total CD attempts (the abstract rounds this down to "~30 million", a minor inconsistency worth noting).
8. **Metric.** Primary metric is threshold-agnostic: `SHD = min_τ SHD(G, Ĝ_τ)/|A^G|`, i.e. best achievable normalized Structural Hamming Distance over any decision threshold on the predicted edge-weight graph. A method's "robustness" score for a violation is this SHD averaged over all severity levels and all 16 data regimes, using a **single HP config chosen to maximize average robustness across ALL 33 violations** (not per-violation-optimal, unlike prior work by Montagna 2023 / Yi 2025) — a deliberate "practical deployment" framing choice.
9. **Ensembling stage.** A separate synthetic training set (same violation/regime distribution) trains meta-models that map the stacked prediction tensor of all `M=10` base methods (`B×M×D×D×L_model`) directly to a predicted graph: `Ensemble_Linear` (single FC layer, no activation), `Ensemble_MLP` (2-layer+ReLU+batchnorm), `Ensemble_Transformer` (learnable embeddings + CLS token). Two non-trainable baselines: `Ensemble_Avg` (unweighted mean of predictions) and `Ensemble_Pareto` (oracle: per-violation best base method — an upper bound, not practically realizable since it needs the ground truth to pick).
10. **Zero-shot real-world transfer check.** All CD methods + ensembles (trained purely on synthetic data) are evaluated on 500 samples from CausalRivers "Random-5" (same authors' own real-world river-discharge benchmark, `G^LSG` labels only, since that's all CausalRivers provides).

### 关键技术细节

- **Violation taxonomy (33 total, 9 families)**: Observational noise (7: add/mul/time/auto/com/shock/real — the "real" variant extracts genuine high-frequency noise from stock-price time series via a Butterworth high-pass filter); Causal sufficiency / hidden confounding (2: instantaneous via exogenous `Z`, lagged via a removed confounder `X_C`); Faithfulness (3: instantaneous path-cancellation, lagged path-cancellation, coefficient-shrink-to-zero); Functional form / nonlinearity (4: monotonic power family — with a full formal proof of monotonicity for β>0 — B-spline trend family, GP-RBF family, composite/symbolic-regression-style chains); Innovation noise (9: same 6 structural variants as observational noise plus uniform/Weibull non-Gaussian blending and unequal-variance); Stationarity (2: bounded coefficient drift vs full graph resampling at change points); Sample size (1: `T∈{76,58,41,23,6}`); Data quality (4: MCAR/MAR/MNAR missingness with linear-interpolation fill, plus "sensor failure" blackout periods with zeroed parent sets); Data scaling (1: alpha-blend raw vs standardized series, targeting the known "standardization shortcut" artifact literature).
- **All 10 methods share the same blind spots.** The appendix's assumption-compliance table (`tab:algo_assumptions`) shows **zero of the 10 methods claim robustness to observational noise, hidden confounding, or non-stationarity** — this is an architecture-level gap across the entire tested field, not a per-method weakness.
- **PCMCI+/SVAR-RFCI structurally cannot beat normalized-SHD = 1 on `G^INST`** because they output undirected instantaneous edges while the metric expects directed ones — an important caveat for anyone reusing their instantaneous-edge output as a "causal effect sign" or "edge stability" signal.
- **Lag misspecification is asymmetric.** Underestimating the true max lag (`↓L`) costs 0.23–0.38 absolute normalized-SHD across all methods; overestimating (`↑L`) costs only 0.01–0.08. Practical recommendation: guess high on `L_model` when uncertain. `CausalPretraining` is immune to this by construction (fixed internal max lag = 3), which the authors themselves flag as "limited implications for real-world applications" since a real system's true lag is rarely known to be exactly 3.
- **Compute cost varies by 3–4 orders of magnitude** across methods for the same 100-sample batch (GVAR ≈0.3–0.4s vs F-PCMCI ≈800–1400s), on a 7-node Slurm cluster (14–24 CPU cores/node); ensemble training used a single RTX 3090. Full reproduction is explicitly stated to need multi-day cluster time.
- **Nonlinear CI-test ablation.** Swapping PCMCI+'s linear partial-correlation test for a nonlinear one (GPDC) measurably improves its robustness to `V_nl,rbf` but still doesn't beat other methods' baseline, at ~100× the compute — justifies excluding nonlinear CI tests from the main sweep.
- **Graph-size ablation (5→7→12 variables, under `V_inno,com`)**: Varlingam and NTS-Notears stay stable or improve slightly with size; GVAR and PCMCI+ degrade substantially — robustness rankings are **not** size-invariant, a caution against extrapolating small-graph rankings to larger real systems.

### 核心结果

- **Best individual method per graph type**: Varlingam for `G^LWCG` (0.408 SHD), Dynotears for `G^INST` (0.515 SHD), GVAR for `G^LSG` (0.330 SHD) — the GVAR/`G^LSG` result is reported as consistent with the same group's earlier real-world CausalRivers benchmark finding.
- **Ensembling result is genuinely nuanced, not uniformly positive**: `Ensemble_Linear` beats every individual method AND beats the oracle `Ensemble_Pareto` on `G^LWCG` (0.362) and `G^LSG` (0.281) — meaning it recombines predictions rather than just picking a winner. But for `G^INST`, **no ensemble beats the oracle Pareto** (0.470*) — reported as an honest negative result rather than smoothed over.
- Ensemble robustness gains concentrate on **missingness violations** (`V_mcar/mar/mnar`) and on **small (5-variable) data regimes** — directly offered as the explanation for why ensembles transfer so well to the 5-variable CausalRivers zero-shot test (best ensemble 0.659 SHD vs best individual method Dynotears 0.715 SHD).
- **Multi-violation stacking** (2 combos tested: `inno,com`+`obs,com`; `conf,inst`+`conf,lag`) reduces robustness further versus either single violation for essentially every method — confirms violations compound, though the authors explicitly caution against over-generalizing from only 2 tested combinations.
- **Metric self-critique.** A dedicated appendix section ("Discussion on Metric Failure Cases") diagrams three scenarios where the 5-point discrete robustness score can mislead: consistently-separated curves (fine), crossing curves within the measured range (score hides a real preference-dependent trade-off), and non-monotonic/volatile curves (score can favor the objectively worse curve). This directly caveats their own headline metric.

### 明确局限

- Only 5 discrete severity levels per violation, with the above self-documented failure modes for crossing/non-monotonic robustness curves.
- **Acyclicity is deliberately not tested** — the authors state they could not define a "meaningful stepwise increase" for it, so cyclic/feedback structures are entirely out of scope.
- Violations are tested **one at a time** in the main study; only 2 arbitrary two-violation combinations appear as an appendix proof-of-concept. Real-world data violates many assumptions simultaneously, and this compounding regime is barely explored here.
- No hidden-confounder-aware or noise-robust CD method is included (all 10 tested methods score ✗ on causal sufficiency) — the study can say current mainstream methods degrade under confounding, but not which architectural fix would help.
- Fully synthetic/semi-synthetic throughout the main study; only real time-series *components* (river discharge, stock-price noise) are borrowed, not full real causal ground truth. The only real-world causal-graph validation is the single 500-sample, 5-variable CausalRivers zero-shot ensemble check.
- Graph sizes tested are small (5, 7, one 12-variable ablation); appendix explicitly cites more-than-quadratic runtime scaling as the reason they didn't push larger.
- **Self-citation nuance**: `CausalPretraining`, one of the 10 tested methods, is by the same first author (Stein et al. 2024), and the real-world validation set (CausalRivers) is also from the same group's prior paper. Doesn't appear to bias results in the group's favor (CausalPretraining is mid-to-low-pack throughout), but is worth flagging for independence assessment.
- LLM usage is disclosed and scoped to prose polishing only (Gemini 2.5 Pro for grammar), not any experimental component.

### 跟我们的关系 (0710-causal-scs-indicator)

The `0710-causal-scs-indicator` idea studies whether **causal-discovery estimator instability itself** (a sliding-window dispersion+jump profile, `(D,J)`, from methods like PCMCI+/LKIF) carries pre-event information for severe convective storms, under a strict event-level falsification protocol against matched non-event controls. TCD-Arena does **not** compete with this at the claim level — it never proposes using CD instability as a predictive/diagnostic signal for anything; it only measures how much CD *output quality* degrades under 33 known, ground-truth-labeled synthetic violations. No novelty threat.

It is however a directly useful **infrastructure/testbed reference** for the idea's Experiment 1 ("Synthetic mechanism gate," which needs matched non-tipping drift, non-iid/autoregressive noise, sample-size/missingness confounds, observation jumps, and real graph-change controls):

- TCD-Arena's `V_stat`/`V_coef` (bounded-drift vs full-resample graph change) is a ready-made "real graph-change" confound generator — exactly the "positive control regime with a known-changing DAG" the v2 review's Missing-evidence-signals row flags as absent from the idea's current design.
- `V_mcar/mar/mnar` + `V_length` cover the "样本量/缺测变化" confound family; `V_obs,*` (7 variants, including real-noise-extraction methodology) covers "观测跳变" — all with pre-calibrated, published severity-level parameters the idea could reuse rather than re-deriving from scratch.
- **PCMCI+ is one of TCD-Arena's 10 benchmarked methods** (LKIF is not covered — a gap). TCD-Arena's finding that PCMCI+ structurally emits undirected instantaneous edges (SHD floor at 1.0) is a concrete methodological caution for the idea's `J` (jump) channel if it uses PCMCI+ instantaneous-edge changes as part of its instability signal — apparent "edge flips" could be a directedness artifact rather than genuine estimator fragility.
- TCD-Arena's lag-misspecification finding (underestimating `L` is catastrophic, overestimating is comparatively benign) is a directly reusable prior for the idea's "UaDB 时间尺度误判" confound channel.
- **Citation-graph proximity**: this paper's only confirmed S2 citer is `Causal-Audit` (arXiv:2604.02488) — already the idea's own closest-prior-work #4 ("假设违背风险打分用于弃权"). The two papers sit in the same emerging assumption-violation-aware-CD niche; TCD-Arena is not currently named in the idea's landscape/baseline list but is worth adding as a tool reference (violation-generator source + PCMCI+ caveat), not as a competing citation.
