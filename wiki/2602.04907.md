- **Year**: 2026.02
- **Authors**: Jianhong Chen (Northeastern University, Mechanical & Industrial Eng.), Naichen Shi (Northwestern University, Industrial Eng. & Mgmt Sci. / Mechanical Eng.), Xubo Yue (Northeastern University, Mechanical & Industrial Eng.)

- **Keywords**: Causal discovery, stochastic differential equations, physics-informed learning, high-dimensional statistics, Lasso support recovery, quasi-likelihood, Lyapunov stability, ODE misspecification
- **One-liner**: Encodes a known (possibly partial/misspecified) ODE as the SDE drift and the unknown causal graph as parent-dependent multiplicative diffusion, with Lasso-type support-recovery theory for stable/unstable regimes and misspecification robustness.

## 精华提炼

### 管线/方法
1. **Input**: a multivariate trajectory `x(t)` on `[0,T]`, discretely observed, plus *partial* mechanistic knowledge — an ODE `g(x,γ,t)` that is fully specified for only a subset of the `p` variables (or none).
2. **Generative model**: multiplicative-noise SDE `dX_t = g(X_t,γ,t) dt + S_A(X_t) dW_t`, where the drift `g` is the known/partial ODE and the diffusion `S_A(X_t) = diag(A_1^T X_t, …, A_p^T X_t)` is a diagonal matrix whose `i`-th entry is a linear (or dictionary-expanded, for the nonlinear extension) function of node `i`'s **parents**. The causal graph lives entirely in which entries of row `A_i` are nonzero — this is the paper's causal notion (Definition 1: `X_j` causally couples to `X_i` "beyond known dynamics" iff the diffusion term of `i` depends nontrivially on `X_j`).
3. **Discretization**: Euler–Maruyama scheme on a grid; define the per-step normalized residual `r_{i,k} = (x_i(k+1) - x_i(k) - g_i(t_k,x_k,γ)Δt_k) / sqrt(Δt_k)`, which is (conditionally) `N(0, (A_i^T x_k)^2)` under the model.
4. **Loss**: negative Gaussian quasi-log-likelihood per node treating `(A_i^T x_k)^2` as the conditional variance — this is ill-posed when a node has no parents (variance → 0), so a **stabilization constant `c>0`** is added: `(A_i^Tx_k)^2 → (A_i^Tx_k)^2 + c`.
5. **Estimator**: row-wise (one Lasso-type problem per node `i`) `ℓ1`-penalized minimization of the stabilized quasi-likelihood over `θ_i` (candidate parent vector), giving algorithm **SCD**.
6. **Optimization**: nonconvex proximal-gradient with monotone backtracking line search; multiple random restarts, keep the lowest objective value; converges to a first-order stationary point (standard nonconvex prox-grad guarantee, no global-optimality claim).
7. **Nonlinear extension**: replace the linear feature `x(k)` with a coordinate-wise dictionary expansion `Φ̄(x(k))` (e.g. `u²`, `sin(u)`), same row-wise estimator and optimizer apply verbatim.
8. **Theory layer** (all under a linear/locally-linearized drift regime): support-recovery guarantees for (a) *stable* SDEs via Lyapunov second-moment stability, (b) *unstable* SDEs via finite-horizon moment localization + a restricted-eigenvalue condition on the predictable Gram matrix, (c) robustness when the ODE drift is *misspecified* by an additive perturbation `δ`, and (d) a feasible/optimal range for the stabilization constant `c`.
9. **Output**: a (possibly cyclic, self-looped) sparse adjacency matrix `Â` — no acyclicity constraint anywhere in the formulation or the theory.
10. **Evaluation**: synthetic linear SDEs (ER-DAGs and directed loop graphs, stable/unstable, `p∈{5,10,15,20}`) against DYNOTEARS / PCMCI / SCOTCH / Neural-GC on SHD/TPR/FDR; semi-synthetic nonlinear Lotka–Volterra and chaotic Lorenz systems (real ODE form + injected synthetic diffusion graph, 2 extra "blind" dims); a real-world case study reconstructing a stochastic SIR model from JHU CSSE COVID-19 data.

### 关键技术细节
- **Causal notion (Definition 1)**: `X_j → X_i` beyond-known-dynamics iff `∂_{x_j} H_i(x,t) ≢ 0`, where `H_i` is `i`'s diffusion-modulation function — an information-flow style definition, explicitly framed as complementary to (not replacing) Granger causality.
- **Model assumptions for well-posedness** (Assumption `SDE_gen`): drift/diffusion locally Lipschitz + linear growth in `x`, square-integrable initial condition with `E[X_0X_0^T] ≻ 0`.
- **Sparsity** (Assumption `sparse`): max parent-set size `s = max_i |pa(i)|` grows sub-linearly in `p` for non-physics-prior nodes.
- **Incoherence** (Assumption `Incoh`): standard Lasso-style condition on the population Fisher-information off-support block, `‖Q*_{V_i^c V_i}(Q*_{V_iV_i})^{-1}‖_∞ ≤ 1-α`.
- **Dependence structure**: the discretely-sampled Euler–Maruyama sequence is assumed geometrically `α`-mixing (Assumption `alpha-mixing-EM`); verified as a worked example for a stochastic Lotka–Volterra skeleton chain, not proven in general.
- **Key theoretical device — Local Restricted Strong Convexity (LRSC)**: because the objective's curvature depends on the unknown ground truth (nonconvex, not globally RSC over a fixed ball as in prior RSC-Lasso work), the stabilization constant `c` is tuned (`c ≥ 9‖θ_i*‖²K²` or similar) to force a curvature lower bound `Δ^T∇²ℓΔ ≥ α_1‖Δ‖² - τr_n‖Δ‖_1²` on the relevant local region — this is the crux that makes a primal-dual-witness (PDW) argument possible despite nonconvexity.
- **Stable-regime theorem** (`Thm: LASSO`): for fixed node `i`, if `λ_n ≳ (K/√c)√(log p/n)` and `n ≳ C·c̃²·s³ log p`, any KKT-satisfying stationary point has no false positives; adding a beta-min (`θ_min ≥ (3√s/(α_1-16τs))λ_n`) condition gives exact support recovery, with success probability `1 - c_1 exp(-c_2 λ_n² n)`.
- **Unstable-regime theorem** (`Thm: lasso_uns`): Lyapunov stability is replaced by (i) a finite-`q`-moment assumption on `X_0` giving finite-horizon boundedness via Grönwall + BDG inequality, and (ii) a restricted-eigenvalue condition directly on the *predictable* Gram matrix `Σ̃ = (1/n)Σ E[X_kX_k^T | F_{k-1}]`; same `n ≳ s³ log p` rate.
- **ODE-misspecification theorem** (`Thm: Lasso_miss`): drift misspecified by additive `δ` per step; recovery persists for `|δ| ≤ δ_0` (an explicit function of `c,B,K,Δt`), with `λ_n` and `n` requirements inflated by `δ`-dependent terms `A_δ, α_δ, D̄_max^(δ)` that reduce to the well-specified constants when `δ→0`.
- **Stabilization-constant trade-off** (`Prop: fesibale_c`): larger `c` stabilizes/well-poses the loss but dilutes the RSC-relevant signal (`Δ_{i,c} ≲ Kc^{-1/2}`); a theory-driven optimal `c*_i ≍ (C_{H,i}Km / (192√3√s λ_n))^{2/3}` is derived by balancing a linear-in-`c` bridge term against a `c^{-1/2}` recoverability term; as `n` grows the admissible upper end of `c` scales like `(n/log p)^{1/3}`, i.e. becomes less restrictive — confirmed empirically in Sec. 5.3.
- **Optimization guarantee**: standard sufficient-decrease proximal-gradient argument (accepted-iterate objective values are monotone, non-summable step differences → 0), giving convergence to a first-order stationary point only — no claim that the specific PDW-constructed estimator is found by the optimizer in practice (multi-restart is a practical heuristic, not proven to find it).
- **Simulation settings**: Euler–Maruyama on `[0,5]`, `Δt=0.01` (500 steps); ER-DAG edge weights `Unif([-0.6,-0.3]∪[0.3,0.6])`; directed loop graphs built from a reciprocal chain (`i↔i+1`) plus two edges into a sink node, weights `Unif(0.3,1.3)`; tuned `c=0.1, λ=3` in low dimension; SCD threshold `[0.20,0.25]`; baseline thresholds hand-adjusted where recommended defaults degenerated (e.g. DYNOTEARS defaults gave zero selected edges in this setting).
- **Real-data pipeline**: JHU CSSE US county-level confirmed-case/death series → state aggregation → daily new cases → `I_t` = rolling sum over an infectious window → `R_t = C_t - I_t`, `S_t = N - I_t - R_t` (`N=100000`) → classical SIR ODE fit via JAX + `diffrax` Tsit5 solver, parameters `(β,γ)` via L-BFGS least squares → this fitted (admittedly misspecified/data-driven) ODE is the drift prior for SCD's diffusion-graph inference on `(S,I,R)`.

### 核心结果
- **Synthetic DAGs, stable, `p=20`**: SCD SHD 69.3 vs. baselines 161.4–241.2; SCD TPR 0.836–0.902 across `p`; SCD FDR as low as 0.111 (`p=5`) vs. baseline FDR 0.356–0.622.
- **Directed loop graphs, unstable, `p=20`**: SCD SHD 13.5, TPR 0.902, FDR 0.274 vs. baselines SHD 58.6–135.6, TPR below 0.17, FDR 0.663–0.858 — the acyclicity-oriented baselines collapse hardest exactly on the cyclic setting SCD is designed for.
- **ODE-misspecification stress test** (5-dim unstable loop system, ODE prior with feedback+self-loops known for only 2/5 vars): at 0% misspecified entries SCD gets SHD 1.1 / TPR 0.903 / FDR 0.025 vs. baselines SHD 8.0–10.2; even at 100% of the ODE-prior entries perturbed, SCD TPR only drops to 0.839 and FDR stays ≈0.025.
- **Stabilization-constant sweep** (stable DAG, `p=5`, `n=500`): recovery is best over a moderate `c` range and degrades at both extremes; at `c=0.8`, growing `n: 500→2000` improves SHD `7.2→5.4` and FDR `0.800→0.508` — direct empirical support for the `n`-dependent admissible-`c` theory.
- **Nonlinear Lotka–Volterra** (2/4 dims ODE-blind at inference): SCD SHD 0.8/1.3 (DAG linear/nonlinear), 1.6/2.7 (loop linear/nonlinear) vs. best baseline SCOTCH 2.5–6.1; SCD TPR 0.81–0.90 with FDR 0.034–0.19.
- **Nonlinear chaotic Lorenz** (2/5 dims ODE-blind): SCD SHD 1.2–3.1 across all 4 settings vs. baselines 6.5–10.9; Neural-GC fails outright (reported NA — "public implementation does not work for our setting").
- **Real COVID-19 SIR reconstruction**: SCD is the only method whose thresholded diffusion graph matches the literature-motivated reference pattern (sparse `(S,I)` block, self-loops, bidirectional `S↔I`, no `R`-driven diffusion); DYNOTEARS degenerates to self-loops only, PCMCI adds a spurious `R→S` edge, Neural-GC gives a near-dense uninterpretable graph, SCOTCH gives a spurious `S↔I↔R` chain.

### 明确局限
- **Author-acknowledged**: restricted to standard ODEs as the physics prior (delay-differential equations, time-varying-parameter systems left as future work); no PDE/spatiotemporal treatment (SPDEs proposed as a "natural next step," not attempted); nonlinear extension is limited to an additive coordinate-wise dictionary (no GP or richer nonlinear function class); no causal-representation-learning extension (latent SDE + observation model, via Itô's lemma, flagged as future direction only).
- **Theory–practice gap**: all recovery theorems require a *linear* (or locally-linearized) drift/diffusion; the nonlinear experiments (Lotka–Volterra, Lorenz) run the same estimator with no matching theoretical guarantee for that regime.
- **`c`-tuning is not fully data-driven**: the theory-optimal `c*_i` depends on per-node unknowns (`‖θ_i*‖`, `C_{H,i}` from an implicit-function-theorem Jacobian bound) that aren't directly estimable, so practical tuning still needs a grid/sweep — exactly what the paper's own Sec. 5.3 experiment does.
- **Real-data validation is qualitative only**: the COVID-19 SIR case study has no quantitative ground-truth graph to score against (SHD/TPR/FDR are not reported there) — only a visual match to a "literature-motivated reference structure"; the ODE prior itself is fit by least-squares and is explicitly acknowledged as approximate/misspecified.
- **Semi-synthetic, not fully real, nonlinear validation**: Lotka–Volterra/Lorenz use the real ODE form but an *injected, synthetic* diffusion/causal graph — there is no fully real multivariate dataset with a known ground-truth causal graph.
- **Scale untested**: experiments cap at `n≈2000`, `p≤20`; no large-`p` scaling study, so behavior at the scale relevant to typical scientific-discovery pipelines (hundreds–thousands of variables) is unknown.
- **Baseline-tuning asymmetry**: some baseline thresholds were manually adjusted away from their published defaults because defaults produced degenerate (zero-edge) outputs in this setting — disclosed transparently, but still a comparison-protocol choice worth flagging.
- I did not find a code/data-availability statement or public repository link anywhere in the text I read; reproducibility currently rests on the (quite detailed) written hyperparameters and full appendix proofs rather than released code.
