# Complete Causal Identification from Ancestral Graphs under Selection Bias (2603.26301)

- **Year**: 2026.03
- **Authors**: Leihao Chen, Joris M. Mooij (Korteweg-de Vries Institute for Mathematics, University of Amsterdam)
- **Venue**: arXiv preprint (cross-listed stat.ME / math.CO / math.PR / math.ST / stat.ML; no venue/proceedings indicated in the source)
- **Keywords**: causal identification, PAGs/MAGs, selection bias, Pearl's do-calculus, FCI algorithm, ancestral graphs, Markov blanket / fixing operation, measure-theoretic causal inference, Berkson's paradox
- **One-liner**: Sound-and-complete, fully measure-theoretic causal calculus and identification algorithm (sIDP) for PAGs/MAGs learned under latent selection bias.

## 精华提炼

### 管线/方法
This is a pure theory paper — there is no empirical pipeline, dataset, or code. The "pipeline" is a chain of graphical/measure-theoretic constructions:

1. **Setup**: causal structure is represented by an isADMG (input/observed/latent/selection-node ADMG), the augmented graph of a structural causal model (iSCM) with a selection mechanism (s-iSCM). Marginalizing out latents gives a MAG/PAG representation (`MAG(·)` operator), generalizing Richardson & Spirtes (2002) to allow exogenous "input" nodes (regime indicators) and selection nodes.
2. **Graph manipulation**: defines *hard* manipulation (do-operator, deletes a node's parents) and *soft* manipulation (adds a regime-indicator parent `I_a`) on iADMGs, iMAGs and iPAGs, and proves the two commute appropriately (Prop. "Manipulations commute").
3. **Graphical separation**: introduces `id`-separation, a generalized d/m-separation for hard-/soft-manipulated iPAGs/iMAGs/iADMGs that correctly handles selection bias (extends Zhang 2008's separation criterion for MAGs to allow selection).
4. **Main soundness/completeness bridge** (Theorems I & II): proves that `id`-separation in a manipulated iMAG or iCOPAG (a fully-oriented PAG) holds **iff** the corresponding conditional independence holds in *every* isADMG (with its selection nodes conditioned on) that the PAG/MAG represents. This is the technical core that lets one reason about a whole Markov-equivalence class at once instead of enumerating member DAGs.
5. **Causal calculus** (Theorem III): translates the separation results into a 3-rule, measure-theoretic version of Pearl's do-calculus (insertion/deletion of observations, actions/observations exchange, insertion/deletion of actions) that is valid for arbitrary (not necessarily discrete) variables and provably *atomically complete* for iMAGs/iCOPAGs.
6. **Identification algorithm sIDP** (Theorems IV & V): a three-step algorithm — (i) reduce to potential-ancestors of the query, (ii) recursively split into "regions" via an assembly tree (generalizing ADMG districts/c-components to PAG "buckets"/"pc-components"), (iii) test each leaf region for "fixability" using a generalized Markov-blanket division step — that is **sound for general PAGs and sound+complete for fully-oriented COPAGs** (i.e., PAGs actually output by the FCI algorithm).
7. **Extensions** (appendix): a hedge criterion for provable non-identifiability, an sCIDP variant for *conditional* interventional queries, and a treatment of undirected graphs / MTP₂ distributions as a degenerate case of MAGs with selection bias.

### 关键技术细节
- Formalism is fully measure-theoretic (arbitrary standard Borel spaces, not just discrete/Gaussian), building on Forré's "transitional probability theory" and a notion of *extended* conditional independence (Dawid's sense) that treats non-stochastic "regime indicator" nodes `I_a` uniformly with random nodes.
- `id`-separation's collider rule is a genuine generalization: a collider `v_{i-1} *→ v_i ←* v_{i+1}` opens the path if `v_i` is a *possible ancestor* of the conditioning set (not just an *ancestor*) when input nodes are involved — needed because, unlike the no-selection-bias case (Zhang 2008; Jaber et al. 2022), the "possible-ancestor-suffices" criterion is *not* sound once do-operators on input nodes are present; the paper's Remark explicitly show sound/complete requires a hybrid potentially-directed/definitely-directed criterion.
- Five model classes are distinguished for identification results: `M(G)` (no positivity), `M⁺(G)` (a.s. identification), `M⁺_c(G)` (positive+continuous kernels → pointwise identification), `M⁺_d(G)` (discrete, strictly positive pmf) and `M⁺_lg(G)` (linear-Gaussian). Soundness is proved at three strengths: up to oracle choice of conditional kernel, almost-surely, and pointwise (for continuous kernels).
- The ID-algorithm "fixing" operation is the technical crux most relevant outside this subfield: for a fixable node `r`, `φ_r(p(x_V|x_W); G) = p(x_V|x_W) / p(x_r | x_{Mb_G(r)})` — i.e., recovering a node's *true* conditional kernel from the joint requires dividing by the density conditioned on `r`'s full **Markov blanket** `Mb(r)` (parents ∪ children ∪ co-parents/spouses), not merely its causal parent set `Pa(r)`. `Pa(r)` alone recovers the *structural/generative* mechanism `p(x_r|x_{Pa(r)})`, which coincides with `p(x_r|x_{Mb(r)})` only when `r` has no children in the graph.
- Selection bias is introduced structurally via latent "selection nodes" `S`; the entire paper's motivation (Introduction, Discussion) is Berkson's paradox: conditioning on a common effect (or, here, on `X_S ∈ S`) can induce spurious dependence between variables that are marginally independent in the unselected population.
- The completeness proof (Theorem V, Sec. "Proofs for §IDalg") is intricate: when sIDP fails on a COPAG, the paper constructs an explicit MAG member and then an isADMG member exhibiting a "hedge" (Shpitser & Pearl-style non-identifiability witness), which requires ~15 pages of case-by-case graphical surgery (discriminating paths, "bucket"/pc-component bookkeeping) — the proof-dependency figures at the end of the paper (Figs. for Thm sep_hsint_mag/pag_mag/idp_sound/idp_complete) map ~35 interlocking lemmas.

### 核心结果
- **Theorem I/II** (separation transfer): `id`-sep in manipulated iMAG ⟺ `id`-sep in every represented isADMG (conditioned on its selection nodes); for general iSOPAGs only the sound direction is proved, but for iCOPAGs (FCI output) both directions hold.
- **Theorem III** (causal calculus): 3-rule do-calculus, sound and *atomically* complete for iMAGs/iCOPAGs.
- **Theorem IV/V** (sIDP): sound for all iSOPAGs (3 soundness strengths depending on model class); sound **and complete** for iCOPAGs — i.e., whenever sIDP fails on an FCI-output PAG, the target effect is provably non-identifiable for at least one member DAG.
- A worked example (Ex. "Identification from an undirected graph") shows that even a purely undirected MAG (no arrowheads at all) can still yield identifiable causal effects once reinterpreted as a MAG-with-selection-bias, connecting to concentration graphs / MTP₂ distributions — a mildly surprising byproduct not central to the main results.
- No numerical/simulation results anywhere in the paper — everything is proved analytically with small illustrative TikZ graph examples (3–6 nodes).

### 明确局限
- Completeness of the causal calculus/sIDP is only established for **iCOPAGs** (fully FCI-oriented PAGs); for general iSOPAGs (e.g., PAGs additionally shaped by background knowledge à la Bryan-Nowak or JCI-style observational+experimental data fusion) only soundness is proved — completeness is explicitly left open.
- The conditional-query variant **sCIDP is only proved sound, not complete**; the authors state completeness "likely requires an appropriate conditional analogue of the Hedge criterion," itself flagged elsewhere in the literature as historically imprecise.
- **No necessary positivity conditions** are known; only sufficient ones are used, and the paper notes identification can fail even when the graphical criterion holds if positivity is violated.
- **Zero empirical content.** No real or simulated data, no time-series setting, no forecasting/prediction framing, no estimator or its statistical efficiency (explicitly flagged as future work), no code/software release.
- Entirely static-DAG/ADMG formalism — no temporal/lagged structure, no dynamical-systems notion of bifurcation, regime change, or "fragility"; PCMCI+-style time-series causal discovery is outside its scope entirely (not cited, not discussed).
- Authors' own "Discussion" section flags that graph-structural validation (e.g., structural Hamming distance) is unreliable and gestures at "effect-level validation" via testable interventional predictions as future work — a methodological opinion, not a worked-out method.

### 跟我们的关系
**Bottom line: this paper is only tangentially useful for the v3 confound problem, and should not be over-cited.** It comes from a different subfield (static causal-graph identification theory under selection bias) and never touches time series, forecasting, PCMCI+, or bifurcation/regime detection — it was presumably surfaced by keyword overlap on "selection bias" / "collider" / "causal conditioning."

Assessing the two questions this deep-lit pass was asked to check:

**(a) Does it help design a cleaner factorial/controlled comparison to isolate `Δ = AUC_conditional − AUC_marginal`?** No. There is no experimental-design content, no simulation methodology, nothing about statistical power, nothing about AUC or any predictive-evaluation metric. Zero transfer to the v3 reviewer's confound (regimes differing in nonlinearity/hidden drivers/effect size/window params, not just conditioning).

**(b) Does it provide a theoretical explanation for "causal conditioning discards marginal-correlation-carried forecasting signal"?** Partially, and the mechanism it foregrounds is arguably the *wrong direction* for the puzzle:
- The paper's central motivating mechanism is **Berkson's paradox / collider bias**: conditioning on a common effect *creates spurious* dependence between variables that are truly independent. The v3 puzzle is the opposite: conditioning on causal parents seems to *destroy real* predictive dependence that a marginal-correlation baseline picks up. Same graphical vocabulary (colliders, selection nodes, `id`-separation), different phenomenon — this paper doesn't supply a mechanism for signal *loss* from parent-set conditioning.
- The one genuinely relevant nugget is buried in the ID-algorithm's "fixing" operation (`φ_r(·) = p(x_V|x_W) / p(x_r | x_{Mb(r)})`, see "关键技术细节" above): it operationalizes the standard graphical-models fact that the **Markov blanket** `Mb(r) = Pa(r) ∪ Ch(r) ∪ spouses(r)` — not the parent set `Pa(r)` alone — is the minimal conditioning set that makes `r` predictively sufficient (screens `r` off from the rest of the graph). If PCMCI+'s causal-parent conditioning set is a strict subset of the effective Markov blanket relevant to forecasting a target variable (e.g., it omits informative "children"/co-parents that a marginal correlation test picks up unconditionally), that is a textbook explanation for `Δ < 0`. But (i) this is general Bayes-net folklore, not a contribution specific to this paper — the natural citation is Pearl (1988) or a graphical-models textbook, not this selection-bias-under-PAGs paper; (ii) the paper never develops or discusses this point — it appears once, in a definition, with zero pedagogical framing; (iii) translating "Markov blanket" from a static DAG to a lag-structured time-series causal graph (PCMCI+'s actual setting, where "children" are future values you may be trying to predict) is nontrivial and this paper gives no guidance on that translation.
- No "partial correlation cancellation" mechanism appears anywhere in the paper.
- The Discussion's complaint about structural-distance-based causal-discovery validation, and its call for "effect-level validation... through testable interventional predictions," is thematically adjacent to the causal-scs-indicator project's own forecasting-AUC-as-validation-signal approach, but is a one-paragraph opinion, not a method to borrow.

**Recommendation**: do not cite this paper as evidence for the v3 theory section. If the Markov-blanket-vs-parent-set argument is used to explain `Δ < 0`, cite the general fact (Pearl 1988 Markov blanket theorem, or a standard PGM/causal-inference textbook) rather than this paper — this paper is a poor fit as "prior art" here since a reviewer familiar with the area would immediately notice the selection-bias framing doesn't match the claimed mechanism. Keep searching specifically for (i) simulation/factorial-design papers comparing conditional vs. marginal-correlation early-warning indicators near bifurcations, and (ii) any paper making the Markov-blanket-vs-causal-parent-set predictive-optimality argument explicitly in a forecasting/prediction (not pure identification) context.
