# Nonparametric conditional local independence testing (2203.13559)

- **Year**: 2022.03
- **Authors**: Alexander Mangulad Christgau, Lasse Petersen, Niels Richard Hansen (Dept. of Mathematical Sciences, University of Copenhagen)
- **Venue**: arXiv preprint (mathematical-statistics theory paper, `amsart` style; funded by Novo Nordisk Foundation)
- **Keywords**: conditional local independence, Local Covariance Measure, double machine learning, counting processes, martingale CLT, cross-fitting, uniform stochastic convergence, continuous-time causal discovery, Granger non-causality (continuous-time analog)
- **One-liner**: Builds a nonparametric double-ML test (X-LCT) for conditional local independence between a counting process and a covariate process, and formally shows classical conditional-independence tests cannot substitute for it.

## 精华提炼

### 管线/方法
- **Object of interest**: a counting process $N$ (e.g. death indicator $N_t=\mathbb 1(T\le t)$) and a real-valued covariate process $X$ (e.g. pension savings), both observed alongside a filtration $\mathcal F_t$ generated by other processes (e.g. $Z$, BMI). Hypothesis $H_0$: $N$ is *conditionally locally independent* (CLI) of $X$ given $\mathcal F_t$ — formally, the $\mathcal F_t$-compensator of $N$ is also its $\mathcal G_t$-compensator, where $\mathcal G_t=\mathcal F_t\vee\sigma(X_s,s\le t)$ (Def. 2.1). This is the continuous-time analogue of Granger non-causality, originally due to Schweder (1970) and formalized graphically by Didelez (2006/2008) and Mogensen & Hansen (2020).
- **Step 1 — residualize $X$**: define a *residual process* $G_t$ of $X_t$ given $\mathcal F_t$ satisfying $E(G_t\mid\mathcal F_{t-})=0$. Two canonical choices: additive residual $G_t=X_t-\Pi_t$ with $\Pi_t=E(X_t\mid\mathcal F_{t-})$ (predictable projection), or quantile residual $G_t=F_t(X_t)-\tfrac12$.
- **Step 2 — define target parameter**: the **Local Covariance Measure** $\gamma_t=E(\int_0^t G_s\,dM_s)$ where $M_t=N_t-\Lambda_t$ is the $\mathcal F_t$-martingale residual of $N$. Prop. 2.2/2.3: under $H_0$, $\gamma\equiv0$; under the alternative, $\gamma_t=\int_0^t \mathrm{cov}(G_s,\bar\lambda_s-\lambda_s)ds$ — i.e. LCM is literally a running covariance between the covariate residual and the "hidden" intensity gap.
- **Step 3 — double-ML estimation with sample splitting/cross-fitting**: on a held-out fold, historically regress $X$ on $(N,Z)$-history to fit $\hat G^{(n)}$, and regress $N$ on $(N,Z)$-history to fit intensity $\hat\lambda^{(n)}$ (Algorithm 1). On the other fold, plug in to compute $\hat\gamma_t^{(n)}=\frac1{|J_n|}\sum_j \int_0^t \hat G_{j,s}d\hat M_{j,s}$. This is a Neyman-orthogonalized score statistic (Sec. 3.1): it is literally $\partial_\beta \ell_t(0)$ of the one-parameter intensity tilt $\bar\lambda_t^\beta=e^{\beta X_t}\lambda_t$, with $X$ replaced by its residual to kill first-order sensitivity to nuisance estimation error.
- **Step 4 — test statistic**: **Local Covariance Test (LCT)** $\hat T_n=\sqrt{|J_n|}\sup_{t}|\hat\gamma_t^{(n)}|/\sqrt{\hat{\mathcal V}_n(1)}$, calibrated against the known distribution of $\sup_t|B_t|$ for standard Brownian motion $B$ (closed-form CDF via Lévy's triple law, Eq. 4.10). **Cross-fitted version (X-LCT)** averages the LCM estimator over $K$ folds (recommended $K=4$–$5$, following Chernozhukov et al. 2018) for full $\sqrt n$-efficiency instead of $\sqrt{|J_n|}$.
- **Estimators used in the worked example**: intensity $\hat\lambda$ via `BoXHED2.0` (gradient-boosted hazard forest, Pakbin et al. 2021); $\hat\Pi$ via penalized linear historical regression (regress $X_t$ on $(Z_s)_{s<t}$ at each grid point, ridge penalty 0.001). Baseline comparator: hazard-ratio test from a (misspecified) marginal Cox model via `lifelines`' `CoxTimeVaryingFitter`.

### 关键技术细节
- **Core asymptotic decomposition** (Eq. 3.9): $\sqrt{|J_n|}\hat\gamma^{(n)} = U^{(n)} + R_1^{(n)}+R_2^{(n)}+R_3^{(n)} + D_1^{(n)}+D_2^{(n)}$. $U^{(n)}$ is the "oracle" martingale term (known nuisances); $R_i$ are nuisance-estimation remainder terms that vanish under a **rate-double-robustness condition**: if $g(n)=\|G-\hat G\|_2\to0$, $h(n)=\|\lambda-\hat\lambda\|_2\to0$, and $\sqrt{|J_n|}\,g(n)h(n)\to0$, then $\sqrt n$-rate is achieved even though each nuisance may converge slower than $\sqrt n$ individually (classic DML product-rate condition, here extended to processes). $D_1,D_2$ carry the signal under the alternative and vanish under $H_0$.
- **New theoretical machinery**: all results hold under **uniform stochastic convergence** over an unrestricted parameter set $\Theta$ (Def. in Appendix B, extending Kasy 2019 and Lundborg/Shah/Peters 2021's Banach-space theory to general — in particular non-separable — metric spaces, esp. Skorokhod space $D[0,1]$), plus a **uniform version of Rebolledo's martingale CLT** (Thm. 7.2, extending Andersen–Borgan–Gill–Keiding 1993) and **uniform chaining/stochastic-equicontinuity** arguments (extending Pollard 1984, Newey 1991) to control the hardest remainder term $R_1^{(n)}$ via sub-Gaussian increments + a chaining lemma.
- **Assumptions**: (i) *UniformBounds* — $\mathcal G_t$-intensity $\bar\lambda_t\le C$ and residual $|G_t|\le C'$ a.s., uniformly over $\Theta$; (ii) *UniformRates* — the DML product-rate condition above; (iii) *UniformVariance* — $\mathcal V^\theta(1)=E(\int_0^1 G_s^2\bar\lambda_s ds)\ge\delta_1>0$ uniformly over $\Theta$ (needed only for the *test*, i.e. for calibrating against $\sup|B_t|$, not for estimator consistency itself).
- **Main results**: Thm 4.2 — LCT has uniform asymptotic level $\alpha$ under $H_0$ (all three assumptions). Thm 4.3 — LCT has power $\to1$ against any local alternative with $\|\gamma\|_\infty \gtrsim |J_n|^{-1/2}$ (root-$n$ local power, matching GCM-type tests). Thm 5.1 — the $K$-fold cross-fitted X-LCT retains uniform level with full $\sqrt n$ efficiency (not just $\sqrt{n/K}$).
- **Simulation DGP**: historical functional linear model $X_t=\int_0^t Z_s\rho_X(s,t)ds+V_t$, $Y_t=\int_0^t Z_s\rho_Y(s,t)ds+W_t$ (Y latent), Cox intensity $\lambda_t^{\rm full}=\mathbb 1(T\ge t)\beta_1t^2\exp(\beta_2Z_t+Y_t)$; local alternatives injected as $Y_t\to Y_t+\rho_0(t)X_t/\sqrt n$ for constant or time-varying $\rho_0(t)\in\{$step, $7\cos(4\pi t)\}$. Code: github.com/AlexanderChristgau/nonparametric-cli-test.

### 核心结果
- Under $H_0$ (zero kernel, correctly specified Cox model), the marginal-Cox hazard-ratio test is already anti-conservative due to $L_2$-penalization; under the constant/Gaussian kernel (**non-collapsibility**: the true model is CLI but the *marginal* Cox model is misspecified) the hazard-ratio test's $p$-values are **completely non-uniform** — i.e. the classical parametric test **fails to control level**, not just power. X-LCT's $p$-values stay close to uniform across all kernels and are only mildly anti-conservative at $n=100$, converging correctly by $n=2000$.
- Local-alternative power: X-LCT has power against all tested $\rho_0\in\{5,10\}$ settings and stabilizes by $n\approx1000$; the hazard-ratio test's power is highly inconsistent (sometimes ~0, sometimes moderate) depending on kernel/settings — because it is testing the wrong (misspecified marginal) model.
- Time-varying alternative $A_{\rm step}$ (coupling flips sign at $t=0.4$) makes $\gamma_t$ **non-monotone**: it is *not* maximal at the endpoint $t=1$. Here the sup-norm X-LCT strictly dominates the endpoint-only test — for $A_{\rm step}$ the endpoint test's power gap is largest, and convergence is slower than for time-constant alternatives (attributed to harder intensity estimation under more complex $X$-dependence).
- The paper's own worked derivation shows **pointwise conditional independence trivializes in continuous time**: for continuous $T$, $N_t=N_{t-}$ a.s., so $N_t$ is a.s. $\mathcal F_{t-}$-measurable and trivially "conditionally independent of anything" given $\mathcal F_{t-}$ — meaning Didelez's own heuristic ("$N_t\perp\!\!\!\perp \mathcal F_{t-}^X\mid\mathcal F_{t-}^{N,Z}$") cannot be used as a *formal* definition of CLI; only the martingale-based Def. 2.1 is well-posed.

### 明确局限
- **No non-stationarity / bifurcation / parameter-drift discussion anywhere in the paper.** I read the full text (4288 lines, Main.tex, single file) and confirm the words "stationary/non-stationary", "bifurcation", "regime shift" do not appear; "regime" is only used for local-alternative sampling regimes. All theory is for i.i.d. replications of a fixed-but-$\theta$-indexed data-generating process — parameter uniformity over $\Theta$ is *cross-sectional* (over a family of DGPs), not *temporal drift within one trajectory*. This is a real gap relative to the idea's fragility-regime question.
- **The word "faithfulness" never appears.** The paper is purely about hypothesis testing, not about learning/identifying local independence graphs or their Markov equivalence classes (that is Mogensen & Hansen's separate line of work, e.g. arXiv:2005.07568, arXiv:2302.12541 — flagged below as follow-up leads).
- Explicitly acknowledged own blind spot (Sec 4.1, mirrors GCM/Shah–Peters 2018): "we cannot expect that the test has power against alternatives... for which the LCM is the zero-function" — i.e. there exist true violations of $H_0$ (real direct influence) for which $\gamma_t\equiv0$ identically, making them **undetectable even asymptotically**. This is a structural/statistical blind spot, not a finite-sample power issue.
- Sample splitting/cross-fitting is *provably necessary even under $H_0$* — the authors explicitly say the usual argument for avoiding splitting in classical CI testing doesn't transfer here "because we condition on information that changes with time" (Sec. 7, Discussion) — i.e. the continuous-time filtration structure itself, not just estimator variance, forces the DML sample-splitting machinery.
- Uniform-boundedness Assumption (i) and variance-non-degeneracy Assumption (iii) are strong; the authors *conjecture* they can be relaxed to moment bounds but explicitly did not pursue it ("at the expense of considerably more technical proofs... we did not pursue this line of research").
- No real data application — Cox/BoXHED simulation only, single hyperparameter-tuning shortcut flagged as a potential (believed negligible) violation of the independence-across-folds assumption.
- Extension beyond counting-process $N$ to general semimartingales/Itô processes is flagged as open, blocked mainly by the practical impossibility of completely observing continuous sample paths (discretization of observation is "beyond the scope of the present paper").

### 跟我们的关系

This paper is the clearest available formalization of "when does a CI-style test structurally mismatch a continuous-time dynamical/event system" — directly relevant to whether PCMCI+'s partial-correlation/CMI conditional-independence tests are structurally, not just power-wise, wrong near fragility/bifurcation regimes.

**(a) Structural mismatch, yes — but via a different mechanism than bifurcation-proximity.** The paper proves two distinct forms of CI/CLI mismatch: (1) *pointwise* CI in continuous time trivializes (degenerates to vacuous truth) for counting-process targets — this doesn't directly hit PCMCI+ since PCMCI+ already discretizes time and conditions on lagged history (closer in spirit to the *discrete-time* Granger-causality analog the paper says CLI generalizes); (2) **non-collapsibility**: testing CI within a marginalized/projected subsystem of a model that truly satisfies CLI in the full system can make a parametric CI-style test (Cox hazard-ratio here) **lose asymptotic level entirely** (not just power) — Fig. 6's ECDF-under-$H_0$ plots show this concretely: p-values become totally non-uniform under model misspecification from marginalization. This is a strong, citable mechanism: if a fragility regime corresponds to effectively marginalizing out fast/hidden variables near a bifurcation, this paper gives a rigorous reason (not just intuition) why a misspecified/marginal CI test would report **false positives/negatives systematically**, not merely lose power. But the paper does **not** discuss non-stationarity or drift toward a bifurcation as a mechanism — that specific link is not made here and remains open for our idea to establish.
- **(b) No faithfulness discussion, but a directly analogous, transferable blind spot exists.** LCM is a covariance-type functional exactly like GCM/PCM; the authors explicitly inherit GCM's known undetectable-alternative problem ($\gamma\equiv0$ under true dependence). Because $\gamma_t=\int_0^t\mathrm{cov}(G_s,\bar\lambda_s-\lambda_s)ds$ is a *running, signed, time-integrated* covariance, sign-changing/oscillating coupling (plausible near a Hopf-type bifurcation with growing oscillatory feedback) is exactly the shape that can integrate toward cancellation at the endpoint — and the paper's own $A_{\rm step}$/$A_{\cos}$ experiments (Fig. "pathsalternative"/"timevaryingrho") *empirically demonstrate* this: an endpoint-only statistic loses power under non-monotone $\gamma_t$, while the sup-norm X-LCT does not. This is a testable, transferable hypothesis for our fragility-regime story: **if fragility manifests as sign-oscillating coupling, a windowed/endpoint CI statistic (as PCMCI+ effectively uses per lag) is structurally more likely to hit a faithfulness-like cancellation than a path-integrated statistic.**
- **(c) Directly reusable controlled-experiment template.** The step/cosine time-varying local-alternative construction ($\rho_0(t)\cdot X_t/\sqrt n$ injected into a Cox intensity) is a ready-made pattern for building our own "approaching-bifurcation" controlled experiment: parameterize coupling strength as a function of "distance to bifurcation" that shrinks/oscillates across the trajectory, then compare a PCMCI+-style windowed CI test against a path-integrated CLI-style statistic (X-LCT or an adapted analogue) under the *same* ground-truth DGP. Public code (github.com/AlexanderChristgau/nonparametric-cli-test) lowers the cost of adapting this.
- **Important caveat for our claim's rigor**: the paper's own valid (X-LCT) test *requires* Assumption (iii) — variance function bounded away from 0 — and Assumption (i) uniform boundedness of intensity/residual. Both are exactly the kind of regularity that plausibly *fails* near a bifurcation (degenerate variance, blowing-up intensity). So even the "conceptually correct" continuous-time-native test is **not proven to remain valid near a bifurcation** — this is genuinely open, not answered by existing local-independence theory. That is precisely the gap our idea would need to fill rather than cite as already solved.
- **Should we cite it?** Yes, as the primary formal reference for "CLI ≠ CI in continuous time, and marginalized/misspecified CI tests lose level under non-collapsibility" — this is a stronger and more precise citation than any of the cross-sectional 7 mechanisms from earlier rounds. No direct claim overlap/collision with our idea (this paper never discusses bifurcations, non-stationarity, or PCMCI+), so citing it is safe and strengthens rather than threatens novelty.

本轮任务：评估 local independence 支线（由 round5 的 2001.06208 触发）是否能回答"PCMCI+ 类条件独立检验在趋近分岔的连续动力系统里是否存在结构性错配"。结论：**部分回答，且指向一个比"趋近分岔"更精确、更可引用的机制**。

- **(a) 结构性错配 vs 有限样本功效不足**：本文没有讨论非平稳/趋近分岔/参数漂移（全文 4288 行通读确认无相关关键词），但提供了两条严谨的、可直接引用的"结构性"（非功效性）错配证据：①连续时间下 pointwise 条件独立会退化为平凡真命题（对 counting process target），经典 CI 检验框架在此意义上不适定；②**non-collapsibility**——在真实满足 CLI 的完整系统的边缘化/投影子系统上做参数化 CI 检验（本文用 Cox hazard-ratio test 示例）会导致检验**完全丧失渐近水平**（Fig. 6 的 ECDF 图显示 p 值在错误设定下彻底非均匀），而不仅仅是功效下降。如果我们的 fragility regime 对应于"趋近分岔时快变量被有效边缘化/聚合"，non-collapsibility 是一个比"趋近分岔"更精确、有正式定理支撑的机制，值得作为核心引用。但"趋近分岔"本身与 non-collapsibility 的因果链条还是需要我们自己建立，本文没有替我们连上。
- **(b) Faithfulness**：本文全文未出现"faithfulness"一词，也不讨论 local independence 图的 Markov 性质/等价类（那是 Mogensen 系列论文的领域，见反引文 2302.12541）。但本文明确继承了 GCM 的"真实存在依赖但统计量恒为零"盲区（$\gamma\equiv0$ under true alternative），这是 faithfulness-violation 的直接类比。更重要的是，本文自己的 $A_\text{step}$/$A_\text{cos}$ 时变实验**实证展示**：当耦合强度随时间变号/振荡时，终点统计量（endpoint test）功效显著下降，而路径积分统计量（sup-norm X-LCT）保持稳健。这给出一个可迁移、可检验的假说：**若 fragility regime 表现为符号振荡的耦合（Hopf 型分岔附近的振荡增长很可能长这样），基于固定滞后窗口的 CI 检验（PCMCI+ 的每个 lag 本质上是这种）比路径积分型统计量更容易撞上类 unfaithfulness 的抵消**。这是本轮最具体、最可直接转化为我们论证的发现。
- **(c) 受控实验设计**：$\rho_0(t)$ 阶跃/余弦时变局部备择假设注入 Cox 强度的仿真设计（公开代码 github.com/AlexanderChristgau/nonparametric-cli-test）是一个可直接复用的模板，用于构造"趋近分岔"受控实验：把耦合强度参数化为"距分岔距离"的函数，在同一 ground-truth DGP 下比较 PCMCI+ 式窗口 CI 检验与路径积分型 CLI 统计量的功效/水平退化曲线。
- **诚实的局限（对我们很重要）**：本文自己证明有效的 X-LCT 检验，其渐近理论要求方差函数一致下界（Assumption 4.1）和强度/残差一致有界（Assumption 2.1）——这两个正则条件在趋近分岔时很可能恰好失效（方差退化到零、强度爆炸）。也就是说，**即使是"概念上正确"的连续时间原生检验，现有理论也没有证明它在趋近分岔区域仍然有效**。这正是我们 idea 需要去填的空白，而不是可以直接引用为"已解决"的结果。
