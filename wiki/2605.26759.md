# Time Series Causal Discovery via Context-Conditioned and Causality-Augmented Pretraining (2605.26759)

- **Year**: 2026.05
- **Authors**: Biao Ouyang, Tengxue Zhang, Zhihao Zhuang, Yang Shu (corresponding), Chenjuan Guo, Bin Yang — East China Normal University
- **Venue**: NeurIPS 2026 (preprint / under submission — uses `neurips_2026` style, `\usepackage[preprint]`)
- **Keywords**: time series causal discovery, pretraining, structural causal model, intervention, causal mixup, root cause identification, Granger causality, out-of-distribution generalization, exogenous variable modeling
- **One-liner**: Pretrains a causal-graph-recovery model (PTCD) on 180K synthetic SCM time series with intervention pretext tasks + causal mixup so it zero-shot/fine-tune generalizes to unseen real-world causal graphs and root-cause tasks.

## 精华提炼

### 管线/方法
- Input: multivariate time series $x \in \mathbb{R}^{T \times C}$. Assumes Granger causality framework, causal sufficiency, no instantaneous effects, time-invariant causal relations, causal minimality, and a continuous nonlinear Additive Noise Model (ANM) — identical assumption set to AERCA (ICLR 2025), the paper's main baseline.
- Step 1 — window segmentation: split into $m$ windows of size $(n{+}1)$ (n lags + current step), giving $x_{window}\in\mathbb{R}^{m\times(n+1)C}$; embed to $s_{intra}\in\mathbb{R}^{m\times(n+1)C\times d}$.
- Step 2 — dual-scale iterative attention: alternate intra-window self-attention (local dynamics) and inter-window self-attention (reshape to $\mathbb{R}^{(n+1)C\times m\times d}$, attend across windows) for $N$ iterations, refining a dual-scale representation $\tilde{s}$.
- Step 3 — window causal graph: an inter-window aggregator reduces $\tilde{s}$ to node features $u$, split into past ($u_{lag}$) and current ($u_t$); MLP-encode each and take the inner product to get link-probability matrix $\tilde{G}$, trained with BCE against ground-truth $G$ (available only on synthetic pretraining data).
- Step 4 — context-conditioned exogenous modeling: models the exogenous prior $p(z)$ as a Gaussian mixture with $K$ components; mixing weights $\boldsymbol{\pi}=\text{Router}(\tilde{s})$ (a routing/gating network conditioned on the local window representation); a variational encoder approximates the posterior $q(z)$ and samples $Z_t$ via reparameterization.
- Step 5 — reconstruction: $\tilde{x}_t = \tilde{g}(\tilde{G}_p\cdot\tilde{f}(x_{lag})) + Z_t$; reconstruction loss is $\ell_2$ error; KL term regularizes $q(z)$ against the mixture prior.
- Step 6 (training-time augmentation) — intervention pretext task: resample $x$ at a random $t_1$, propagate forward through the (known, synthetic) causal dynamics over $[t_1,t_2]$ to build an intervened series $x^{do}$; derive binary per-window intervention labels; train a classifier to predict them ($\mathcal{L}_{do\_window}$, BCE) plus a contrastive term aligning intervened vs. original node representations ($\mathcal{L}_{do\_contrast}$, InfoNCE-style with temperature $\tau$).
- Step 7 (training-time augmentation) — time series causal mixup: sample two series' latent reps and causal graphs, mix both with the *same* Beta-sampled coefficients so representation and graph move together, then supervise the mixed graph prediction with BCE.
- Step 8 — pretraining loss: $\mathcal{L}_{pre}=\mathcal{L}_{recon}+\lambda_G\mathcal{L}_G+\lambda_{KL}\mathcal{L}_{KL}+\lambda_{do}\mathcal{L}_{do}$ (all $\lambda=0.6$), trained on 180,000 synthetic series generated via a RHINO-style 4-step simulator (ER/SF topology → GMM exogenous noise → MLP-driven temporal simulation → burn-in/stabilization).
- Step 9 — fine-tuning: only reconstruction + KL terms (no ground-truth graph or intervention signal in real data). Two downstream tasks: (a) **Causal Discovery** — fine-tune on CausalRiver-Bavaria, evaluate AUROC on CausalRiver-Eastern-Germany (5 relation-type subsets); (b) **Root Cause Identification** — fine-tune/eval on SWaT and MSDS, score channels by z-score of exogenous residual (min over mixture components) against normal-period statistics, threshold via Streaming Peaks-Over-Threshold (SPOT), rank by Recall@K.
- Output: a per-window causal link-probability matrix (AUROC-evaluated) and/or a ranked list of anomalous/root-cause channels for an incident window.

### 关键技术细节
- Backbone: 4 alternating intra/inter-window attention blocks, standard scaled-dot-product attention; no head-count/width details given beyond block count. Total params: 877.24K — a small model relative to typical pretrained foundation models.
- Gaussian mixture components $K=10$ (swept in sensitivity analysis). Loss weights $\lambda_G=\lambda_{KL}=\lambda_{do}=0.6$, reported as jointly grid-optimal — all three landing on the exact same value is a mild red flag for grid coarseness rather than a genuine joint optimum.
- Optimizer Adam, lr $10^{-3}$ pretrain / $10^{-4}$ fine-tune, early stopping patience 30 epochs, MinMax scaler preprocessing (following AERCA). Trained on a single NVIDIA A800 80GB GPU.
- Synthetic corpus: 1,000 causal graphs per node-count (5–40 nodes, ER or SF topology) × 5 series/graph = 180,000 training series (length $T{=}100$ after 100-step burn-in); val/test = 370 series each with causal graphs strictly disjoint from training.
- Real benchmarks: CausalRiver (666 Eastern-Germany + 494 Bavaria stations, 2019–2023, 15-min resolution, 5 relation categories: Close / Root cause / Random+1 / Confounder / Random at 3- or 5-node subgraphs). Root-cause: SWaT (51 sensors, water-treatment testbed, attack scenarios, downsampled every 10s) and MSDS (10 channels, OpenStack fault injection, downsampled every 5 steps).
- Two appendix theorems: (1) **Causal Identifiability via Intervention Detection** — a density-ratio-estimation argument (same logic as GAN-optimal-discriminator/NCE) showing the optimal observational-vs-intervened discriminator is a monotonic function of the true conditional $P(x_i\mid Pa(x_i))$, i.e. minimizing $\mathcal{L}_{do\_window}$ forces recovery of true parents. (2) **Geodesic Consistency of Causal Probability** — a Manifold-Mixup-style argument that mixing in a learned latent space (not raw input space) approximates geodesic interpolation and implicitly bounds the Jacobian/Lipschitz constant of the causal-graph predictor.
- Baselines: classical (PCMCI, VarLiNGAM, DYNOTEARS, VAR, CDMI, TCDF, CUTS+) and pretrained (Causal Pretraining CP, GRU/Transformer variants) for causal discovery; ε-Diagnosis, RCD, CIRCA, AERCA for root-cause identification.
- Code released at an anonymous.4open.science link (standard NeurIPS double-blind anonymization; not yet verifiable as a permanent public repo).

### 核心结果
- Causal discovery avg. AUROC (10 CausalRiver subsets): PTCD **0.85** vs. next-best CP-Transformer 0.80, CP-GRU 0.78, classical VAR/VarLiNGAM/CDMI ≈0.76–0.78, PCMCI 0.69; TCDF/CUTS+/DYNOTEARS sit near chance (0.50–0.57).
- Largest PTCD margin is on the **Confounder** subsets (0.81/0.79 vs. next-best ≈0.71/0.72) — notable because PTCD's own SCM assumes causal sufficiency (no latent confounders), so this empirical gain is not licensed by its stated theory.
- Root-cause identification: MSDS Avg@10 PTCD 0.929 vs. AERCA (prior SOTA) 0.896; SWaT Avg@10 PTCD 0.440 vs. AERCA 0.342 — a larger relative gain on the harder SWaT benchmark.
- Ablations: removing exogenous-mixture modeling hurts most on both tasks (CD avg AUROC 0.85→0.81; MSDS Avg@10 0.929→0.694); removing the intervention pretext hurts most on confounded/root-cause-heavy settings — consistent with the paper's framing of exogenous modeling + intervention as its two novel ingredients over AERCA/CP.
- Efficiency: PTCD zero-shot inference 0.01s vs. TCDF 17.19s / CUTS+ 14.19s for a full from-scratch fit — but this is a pretrained-forward-pass vs. train-from-scratch comparison (different paradigms), not a same-cost apples-to-apples number.
- Zero-shot avg. AUROC is only reported once, in the efficiency table (0.74), 11 points below the fine-tuned number (0.85) used in the headline Table 2 — i.e. the harder OOD/zero-shot regime the abstract emphasizes is under-reported relative to the fine-tuned regime.

### 明确局限
- Authors' own stated limitation (Appendix "Limitations"): the ANM assumption cannot capture non-additive disturbances (multiplicative noise, nonlinear entanglement); flagged as future work.
- No real dataset has a ground-truth causal DAG for fine-tuning beyond CausalRiver's expert-defined relation categories; SWaT/MSDS only have anomaly/root-cause labels, so root-cause evaluation never validates the recovered causal graph itself, only the downstream z-score ranking.
- Causal sufficiency + time-invariant-relations assumptions are carried over unchanged from AERCA; the "generalization" contribution is transfer *across* different but each internally time-invariant graphs, not robustness to regime-shifting/non-stationary causal structure *within* one series.
- All synthetic pretraining graphs are ER/SF topologies with 2-layer/64-unit MLP dynamics and GMM noise — a fairly narrow generative family for a paper whose central claim is cross-domain generalization to real river and industrial data.
- Fine-tuning-phase loss equation notation is inconsistent with the rest of the paper: it is written as $\lVert \tilde{s}_t - s_t\rVert_2$ (representation-space symbols) where the reconstruction loss everywhere else is defined on $x_t/\tilde{x}_t$ (observation space) — likely a leftover typo, but it means the fine-tuning objective as literally written does not match the pretraining reconstruction loss it claims to specialize.
- The two appendix "theorems" re-derive standard density-ratio-estimation and Manifold-Mixup arguments for this setting; they show the *loss functions* target the right quantities under the assumed generative model, not that the *finite, trained network* actually attains those optima — the ablation table, not the theorems, is the real evidence for the method's claims.
- No confidence intervals/std reported for the main causal-discovery AUROC table (Table 2/ablation), unlike the root-cause table which does report ± std — makes the causal-discovery gains harder to judge for significance.

### 跟我们的关系
- **No claim-level collision with `0710-causal-scs`.** That project asks whether *instability* of a rolling causal-effect estimate (dispersion channel: rolling variance/CI width; jump channel: normalized first-differences/change-points/sign-flips) is a pre-event diagnostic for severe-convective-storm onset — i.e., it uses estimator (in)stability itself as the signal. PTCD's claim is about the *accuracy/transferability* of a single point-in-time causal-graph estimate across datasets. Different research questions; safe from a novelty-collision standpoint.
- **Transferable idea 1 — confounding construction.** PTCD's CausalRiver "Confounder" subset (a multi-child node removed to simulate permanent hidden confounding) is methodologically close to our hidden-driver / shared-upstream-parameter setup; worth a look if we need a harder synthetic confounding scenario than the current paired saddle-node design.
- **Transferable idea 2 — intervention-detection-as-identifiability argument.** PTCD's Appendix theorem (predicting whether a window was intervened on is equivalent to recovering true parents) is a reusable theoretical framing for *why* a jump/change-point-style classifier (real-vs-perturbed window) should carry causal information — relevant if we ever want to argue for the jump channel's validity beyond empirical AUROC gating.
- **Transferable idea 3 — SPOT thresholding.** PTCD's root-cause scoring (z-score of exogenous residual, thresholded via Streaming Peaks-Over-Threshold) is the same recipe class as our dispersion/jump-channel + AUROC-gate design. SPOT is a concrete, already-validated off-the-shelf alternative to whatever fixed/percentile threshold scheme the pilot currently uses for alarm declaration.
- **Possible estimator swap-in, with a caveat.** PTCD offers a pretrained, OOD-generalizing, ~0.01s-per-window causal-link estimator — a candidate replacement for the PCMCI+/VAR proxies our pilot currently uses (per `EXPERIMENT_LOG.md`, our real-PCMCI+ Regime-1 causal channel sits at chance, 0.482 AUC). **However**, PTCD's SCM explicitly assumes *time-invariant causal relations* — the opposite of what our indicator is trying to detect (causal-structure destabilization near a tipping point). PTCD was pretrained/validated only on cross-series generalization (different but each-internally-stationary graphs), never on within-series non-stationarity or regime switching. Plugging it in as a rolling-window estimator would be testing it fully outside the regime its authors validated, so any instability signal it produces near an event would be unvalidated behavior, not a documented capability — flag this explicitly if it's ever proposed as a drop-in replacement.
- **No venue/dataset overlap** — PTCD never touches severe-weather, tipping-point, or early-warning-signal framing; it is a pure causal-discovery-architecture paper, not a competing diagnostic-indicator paper.
