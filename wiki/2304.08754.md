# W-MAE: Pre-trained weather model with masked autoencoder for multi-variable weather forecasting (2304.08754)

- **Year**: 2023.04
- **Authors**: Xin Man, Chenghong Zhang, Jin Feng, Changyu Li, Jie Shao — University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC); Institute of Plateau Meteorology & Institute of Urban Meteorology, China Meteorological Administration
- **Venue**: arXiv preprint (cs.LG / cs.AI / physics.ao-ph); manuscript formatted in Springer Nature journal style (`sn-jnl` class), no confirmed journal publication found
- **Keywords**: weather forecasting, masked autoencoder, self-supervised pre-training, Vision Transformer, ERA5, FourCastNet, precipitation forecasting, ensemble forecast
- **One-liner**: MAE-style self-supervised pre-training on ERA5 fields (ViT encoder + a decoder cheapened for meteorological image size) stabilizes multi-step forecasting and lifts FourCastNet's precipitation ACC by ~20% when transplanted into it.

## 精华提炼

### 管线/方法

W-MAE is a three-stage pipeline, all operating on ERA5 reanalysis snapshots treated as multi-channel 2D images (each of the 20 atmospheric variables is one channel of a `721 × 1440` lat-lon grid):

1. **Task-agnostic pre-training (self-supervised, no labels)**. Each ERA5 sample is split into non-overlapping square patches. A fraction of patches (mask ratio `m`) is randomly dropped. Only the visible patches are linearly projected, given sinusoidal positional encodings, and fed through a standard ViT encoder (multi-head self-attention + MLP blocks, pre-LN, residual connections — Eq. 1-3 in the paper). The full patch sequence (visible + mask tokens re-inserted at their original grid positions) is then passed to the **W-MAE decoder**, which reconstructs the masked pixels. Loss = per-pixel MSE, averaged only over masked patches.
2. **Fine-tuning for multi-variable forecasting**. The pre-trained encoder+decoder is fine-tuned autoregressively to learn the one-step map `X(k) → X(k+1)` at Δt = 6h, then rolled forward iteratively at inference to produce multi-day forecasts.
3. **Fine-tuning for precipitation forecasting**. Same backbone, fine-tuned to map the previous timestep's 20-variable state to 6-hour cumulative total precipitation (TP), a sparser and non-linearly-derived diagnostic variable.

The one architectural contribution over vanilla MAE is the **decoder's attention scope**. Standard MAE decoders run full self-attention over all `N` patches of the (flattened, 1D) patch sequence; because a meteorological image (`721×1440`) is ~3× the pixel count of a typical CV image (`224×224`), this is expensive. W-MAE instead reshapes the 1D patch sequence back into its native 2D `(X, Y)` patch grid and restricts self-attention to the second (longitude) axis only — i.e., attention rows are computed per-latitude-band rather than globally. This is reported to give a 1.2× per-epoch speedup and ~8% memory reduction versus the vanilla MAE decoder at comparable reconstruction quality.

A separate but important pipeline fact: the same MAE pre-training scheme is **transplanted onto FourCastNet's own backbone** (its AFNO token-mixer blocks replace self-attention in both encoder and decoder for the large-data W2 setting, since patch counts at `8×8` patch size exceed where self-attention stays practical) — i.e., the paper's real claim is "MAE pre-training as a task-agnostic wrapper" rather than "our specific decoder is essential."

### 关键技术细节

- **Data**: ERA5, 20 atmospheric variables across 5 vertical levels + 1 integrated column variable (surface: `U10, V10, T2m, sp, mslp`; 1000 hPa: `U,V,Z`; 850 hPa: `T,U,V,Z,RH`; 500 hPa: `T,U,V,Z,RH`; 50 hPa: `Z`; integrated: `TCWV`). Global `0.25°×0.25°` grid, 6-hour sampling.
- **Two training-data regimes**, each with its own architecture:
  - **W1** (small-data, 2 years: train 2015, val 2016-17, test 2018): patch size `16×16`; ViT encoder depth=16 dim=768; decoder depth=12 dim=512; standard self-attention decoder.
  - **W2** (large-data, 37 years: train 1979-2015, val 2016-17, test 2018, matching FourCastNet's split): patch size `8×8`; encoder depth=12 dim=768; decoder depth=6 dim=512; self-attention swapped for **AFNO blocks** (borrowed from FourCastNet) in both encoder and decoder because the `8×8` patch count is too high for attention to stay effective.
- **Optimizer**: AdamW, β₁=0.9, β₂=0.95, weight decay 0.05.
- **Pre-training epochs**: 600 (W1), 200 (W2) — the paper explicitly flags W2 as **not yet converged** at 200 epochs.
- **Fine-tuning epochs**: 120 (W1) / 100 (W2) for multi-variable; separate runs for precipitation.
- **Mask ratio**: ablation shows loss-optimal ≈0.6, but 0.75 is chosen in practice — comparable reconstruction quality, 8% less memory, ~4 min/epoch faster.
- **Baseline**: FourCastNet (Pathak et al. 2022), compared two ways — (a) authors' own from-scratch FourCastNet run at FourCastNet's published epoch count (150) but *without* its second-round error-accumulation training, for the multi-variable and W1-precipitation comparisons; (b) the officially released FourCastNet checkpoint (which *does* include the second round of training) for the W2/37-year precipitation comparison — the paper flags this inconsistency itself in a footnote.
- **Metrics**: latitude-weighted Anomaly Correlation Coefficient (ACC) and RMSE (Rasp et al. formulation), evaluated at fixed forecast lead-time steps against 2018 held-out initial conditions (`N_f` ranging 36-180 depending on variable's decorrelation time).
- **Ensemble forecast**: 100-member Gaussian-perturbed initial conditions (σ=0.3, FourCastNet protocol), applied to Z500 only.
- **Compute**: 8× NVIDIA Tesla A800 GPUs; ~63 min/epoch (W-MAE) vs ~55 min/epoch (FourCastNet) for multi-variable fine-tuning, but W-MAE needs fewer total epochs (100 vs 150) → ~23% wall-clock savings (105h vs 137h). Inference: 320 ms per 24h-forecast step on one A800.
- **Code/data**: claimed at `github.com/Gufrannn/W-MAE` (not independently verified in this review — not fetched, per this pass's scope).

### 核心结果

| Setting | Result |
|---|---|
| Multi-variable, 2yr data | W-MAE stable through the full forecast horizon; FourCastNet(2yr) degrades sharply after ~24h and becomes unusable beyond that. |
| Multi-variable, 37yr data | W-MAE still beats FourCastNet(37yr) and stays stable, but W-MAE(37yr) underperforms W-MAE(2yr) beyond ~130h lead time — attributed by the authors to W2's pre-training not having converged (200 vs 600 epochs). |
| Precipitation (TP), 37yr, 6h lead | ACC 0.996 (W-MAE) vs 0.804 (FourCastNet, official 2-round checkpoint) — a ~20% gap. |
| Precipitation (TP), 37yr, 5-day lead (20th step) | ACC 0.886 (W-MAE) vs 0.234 (FourCastNet) — FourCastNet effectively collapses at long lead times for precipitation. |
| Precipitation (TP), 2yr data | FourCastNet(2yr) produces NaN predictions beyond 108h lead time; W-MAE does not. |
| Pre-training transplanted onto FourCastNet | +30% precipitation ACC (2yr data) and +20% (37yr data) vs FourCastNet without pre-training — the headline "20% average ACC improvement" claim in the abstract comes from this transplant experiment, not from W-MAE vs FourCastNet directly. |
| Ensemble (Z500, E=100, σ=0.3) | Ensemble mean is slightly *worse* than the single control forecast at 1-day lead, but clearly better beyond 5 days — standard ensemble-forecasting behavior, reproduced here. |
| Efficiency | W-MAE fine-tuning ~23% faster wall-clock than FourCastNet for multi-variable (105h vs 137h @ 8×A800); inference 320ms/24h-step. |

### 明确局限

- **Coarse vertical resolution**: only 5 pressure/height levels (surface, 1000, 850, 500, 50 hPa) plus one integrated column variable — far coarser than what a genuine isobaric-level 4D (level × lon × lat × time) analysis would need; no boundary-layer-resolving levels, no dense mid-tropospheric sampling.
- **Coarse horizontal resolution and global medium-range scope**: `0.25°` (~28 km) global grid, 6-hour steps, evaluated out to multi-day lead times — this is a medium-range global NWP-emulator, not a short-fuse (0-6h), storm-scale (km-resolution) regional severe-convection nowcasting system.
- **No causal or interpretability analysis whatsoever.** It is a pure end-to-end representation-learning approach (ViT + MAE); there is no attempt to explain *what* structure the model has learned, no spatial/temporal causal-structure analysis, no lag-structure analysis. This is exactly the "black box" class of model the causal-scs-indicator idea is positioned against.
- **W2 (the 37-year, higher-data model) is explicitly admitted to be under-converged** at submission time — the reported large-data numbers are a lower bound on W-MAE's own ceiling, and the counter-intuitive W2-worse-than-W1-at-long-lead result is explained away rather than resolved.
- **Inconsistent baseline checkpoint** across experiments (self-trained single-round FourCastNet vs officially released two-round-trained FourCastNet) — flagged honestly in a footnote, but it means the precipitation "20%" and "0.994 vs 0.804" headline numbers are not a fully controlled apples-to-apples comparison.
- **No extreme-event-conditioned evaluation.** ACC/RMSE are aggregate skill scores; there is no CSI/POD/FAR or any severe/extreme-precipitation-specific skill metric, no case studies on individual severe storms — precipitation is evaluated as a generic diagnostic variable, not as a severe-weather warning signal.
- **Single comparator baseline.** GraphCast and Pangu-Weather are discussed in related work as contemporaries but never benchmarked against, despite being clearly stronger models by the time of writing.
- **No uncertainty quantification beyond a simple Gaussian-perturbation ensemble** — no comparison to bred-vector or ensemble-DA-style initial-condition perturbation, no calibration analysis of the ensemble spread itself.

### 跟我们的关系

No claim-level collision with `causal-scs-indicator`: W-MAE does no causal inference, no spatial/temporal causal-structure analysis, and does not target severe convective storms or short-fuse nowcasting at all — it is a global, medium-range, multi-day NWP-emulator. Nothing here competes with the (D,J) causal spatiotemporal-feature-analysis angle, and nothing here needs to be cited defensively.

Where it *is* useful:

- **It is a concrete, well-documented instance of the exact comparator class** the idea's true motivation (see `causal-scs-indicator-true-motivation` memory) needs: a black-box model trained end-to-end on raw multi-level atmospheric fields (ViT+MAE here; the idea's roadmap calls for CNN/U-Net/GBT-class comparators first, then more sophisticated architectures). It directly substantiates the premise that "AI ingesting raw fields end-to-end" is the mainstream competing paradigm — this paper is itself evidence for that framing, not a refutation of it.
- **Its "5 vertical levels as stacked channels" encoding** is a real precedent for how to feed a multi-level atmospheric field into a ViT/CNN-style model — relevant groundwork for whatever ML-comparator stage (P1 onward in the idea's roadmap) eventually needs to ingest the same 4D (pressure-level × lon × lat × time) input the causal (D,J) pipeline sees. Its resolution (5 levels, 0.25° global) is far coarser than the idea's target (HRRR-class 3km isobaric levels), so it is not directly reusable, but the channel-stacking pattern is.
- **Self-supervised MAE pre-training is a candidate technique to name in the idea's model-zoo roadmap** (per the fifth clarification memory, which explicitly calls for the roadmap to acknowledge a trajectory toward more sophisticated architectures like DiT) — MAE-style masked reconstruction pre-training is a lighter-weight, well-validated intermediate step between simple baselines and DiT that could be worth naming as a rung on that ladder.
- **The precipitation-forecasting failure mode is a useful citable fact**: FourCastNet's NaN blowups and collapse at long lead times for precipitation specifically (the most convection-relevant diagnostic variable) is direct evidence that even strong black-box AI weather models struggle most exactly on the variable class (precipitation/convection) the idea cares about — supports the "black-box models may not learn all the useful structure, especially for convective/precipitation phenomena" premise without needing to make that argument from scratch.
- **No overlap risk, no need to cite as related work in a novelty sense** — but worth citing as landscape/motivation evidence ("modern AI weather models are trained end-to-end on raw fields; even strong ones like FourCastNet show specific fragility on precipitation") if the proposal's introduction wants a citable example beyond Flora et al.
