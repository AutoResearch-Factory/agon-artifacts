# Domain adaptation under structural causal models (2010.15764)

- **Year**: 2020.10 (JMLR 22, 2021)
- **Authors**: Yuansi Chen, Peter Bühlmann — Seminar for Statistics, ETH Zürich
- **Venue**: Journal of Machine Learning Research (JMLR)
- **Keywords**: domain adaptation, structural causal models, anticausal/causal learning, conditionally invariant components (CIC), domain invariant projection (DIP), conditional invariance penalty (CIP), label shift, target risk guarantees
- **One-liner**: Closed-form target-risk theorems show DA invariance-matching estimators (DIP/CIP/CIRM) only beat naive pooling when causal direction and intervention type are correctly matched — otherwise they tie or lose.

## 精华提炼

### 管线/方法

Setup: M ≥ 1 labeled source environments + 1 unlabeled target environment, all generated from **linear SCMs** that share the same structural matrices (B for X↔X, b_v for Y→X, b_h for X→Y) but differ only in an **intervention term** a^(m) added to the noise (mean-shift: a+ε; variance-shift: a⊙ε). This isolates "what changes between environments" to a single controllable knob, which is what makes closed-form risk comparison possible.

Estimator family compared (all population/oracle versions first, then finite-sample plug-in versions with regularization):
- **OLSTar / Causal** — oracles (use target labels or the true SCM coefficient b_h).
- **OLSSrc^(m) / SrcPool** — non-causal baselines, source-only, no invariance machinery at all.
- **DIP** (domain invariant projection, Baktashmotlagh et al. 2013) — matches a moment (mean / std / MMD) of a linear projection of X between **one** source env and the target. Uses target covariates but not target labels, and uses no causal/environment structure beyond one source-target pair.
- **CIP** (conditional invariance penalty, Gong et al. 2016 / Heinze-Deml & Meinshausen 2017 style) — the "causal-conditioning" analogue: matches **E[X|Y]** (or its linear-projection surrogate) across **M ≥ 2 source environments**, i.e. it looks for a (d−p)-dimensional subspace of X whose conditional distribution given Y is invariant across environments, where p = rank of the span of the M−1 pairwise intervention-difference vectors. Uses only source data.
- **CIRM** (conditional invariant residual matching) — the paper's new estimator: runs CIP first to build a scalar proxy for Y, regresses out that proxy to build a residual, then applies DIP-style covariate matching on the residual. Designed to survive Y-interventions where DIP alone fails.
- Weighted/matching-penalty/mixed-causal variants (DIPweigh, CIRMweigh, DIP/CIP/CIRM-std/-std+/-MMD, DIP⋄/CIP⋄/CIRM⋄ for mixed graphs) — all formalized with population + finite-sample forms and proofs in the appendix.

Evaluation pipeline: (1) derive **exact population target-risk formulas** for every estimator under a stated Assumption (linear SCM + specific intervention type); (2) specialize to isotropic-noise Gaussian corollaries to get clean risk expressions in terms of ‖b_v‖², dimension d, environment count M, intervention magnitude τ; (3) simulate with random SCM coefficients (100 repeats) and report **win-rate** (fraction of repeats where estimator A beats estimator B) via scatterplots with a diagonal reference line; (4) validate on MNIST with synthetic pixel/rotation/translation interventions on CNN last-layer features (assumption-violating, nonlinear); (5) validate on Amazon Review (15 product categories as environments, TF-IDF features, d=482) where neither the causal structure nor intervention type is known.

### 关键技术细节

- **Causal-direction flip (the central mechanism, Examples 1–3 + Thm 1)**: In the *causal* setting (X → Y, b_h ≠ 0, b_v = 0), DIP is *provably worse* than OLSSrc/Causal — using target-covariate information about a cause is actively harmful once you're predicting effect-from-cause. In the *anticausal* setting (Y → X, b_v ≠ 0, b_h = 0) with no Y-intervention, Theorem 1 gives DIP's exact target risk = σ_Y²/(1 + σ_Y² b_v^T Σ_X^(-1/2) G_DIP Σ_X^(-1/2) b_v), where G_DIP is a rank-(d−1) projection orthogonal to the single source-target intervention-difference direction u; this is always ≤ Causal's risk (σ_Y²) and, under random Gaussian interventions, within O(1/d) of the oracle OLSTar. So the *same* estimator (DIP) is a guaranteed win or a guaranteed loss purely as a function of causal direction — nothing about DIP itself changes.
- **CIP's environment-vs-dimension boundary (Thm 2 + Cor 2, mirrors IRM's env≤dim mechanism but for a different, closed-form estimator)**: CIP needs the M−1 source-intervention-difference vectors to span a p-dimensional subspace with p ≤ d−1 (Eq. 15) and needs the target intervention to lie in that span (Eq. 16) — i.e. CIP needs "enough but not too many" environments relative to d. Under i.i.d. Gaussian interventions, Corollary 3 gives an explicit **non-asymptotic** high-probability sample-complexity constant: for d ≥ 6M, with probability ≥ 1 − 2exp(−d/32) − 2exp(−M/32), the CIP/CIRM projection bias term shrinks like (1 − c(M−1)/d)‖b_v‖² for CIP and (1 − c/d)‖b_v‖² for CIRM^(m). This is an *independent, non-IRM derivation* of the same qualitative "M vs d" boundary that Rosenfeld et al. (2010.05761) proved for IRM's optimization landscape — here it falls out of pure linear algebra + concentration for a constrained-least-squares estimator, with a concrete constant (d ≥ 6M) rather than an asymptotic statement.
- **Important conceptual distinction this paper surfaces (relevant to bounding mechanism #1's scope)**: in CIP/CIRM, "more environments" is used as an **averaging/estimation resource** — more environments strictly *increase* the span dimension p and *improve* the risk bound (more data to estimate the invariant subspace, no power decay). This is the opposite operation from CI-testing-style "causal conditioning" (as in ICP/PCMCI+), where conditioning on **more variables within a single test** shrinks the effective sample size per stratum and *decreases* test power. The paper's math makes this distinction sharp: CIP has no p-values or hypothesis tests anywhere — it is a deterministic constrained-optimization problem, so there is no "power" to decay. Any catalog entry that reads "conditioning-set-size degrades performance" should specify whether "conditioning" means (a) additional CI-test conditioning variables within one test (power ↓, mechanism #1's actual regime) or (b) additional environments used for averaging (bias ↓, this paper's regime) — conflating the two mis-applies the boundary condition.
- **Assumption-violation collapse toward near-chance win-rate (new empirical data point, not in the 6-mechanism catalog yet)**: Simulation (iii) (CICs exist, M=14, d=20): CIRMweigh beats SrcPool in ~95–100/100 repeats. Simulation (vi) (same d, M, but *no* CICs — X-interventions span the whole space): CIRMweigh beats SrcPool in only **64/100** repeats — barely above coin-flip, and the paper's own explanation is that this residual 64% is likely due to CIP picking up a "softly" invariant combination by luck, not a real guarantee. Example 4 (mixed causal/anticausal graph, naive CIP applied without knowing parent/descendant roles): CIP's target risk is *exactly tied* with SrcPool (0.100 = 0.100) — zero benefit, not graceful degradation. This is a concrete, quantitative instance — in a *different* estimator family (linear DA/CIRM, not CI-testing/PCMCI+, not anchor regression, not IRM) — of the qualitative "fragility regime ⟹ causal-conditioning collapses toward chance while the structure-agnostic baseline is unaffected" pattern central to the round's research question. It strengthens the claim that this collapse is estimator-family-agnostic rather than an artifact of any one method.
- **Matching-penalty/intervention-type mismatch nulls the estimator (Sec 6.1.2, Simulations viii–ix; broadens mechanism #4 to a new instantiation)**: when the true intervention is a **variance shift** but DIP/CIRM only enforces a **mean-matching** constraint, the constraint is trivially satisfied (both source and target means are unchanged) and DIP-mean/CIRM-mean-weigh become numerically identical to OLSSrc/SrcPool — zero benefit, not partial benefit. Only DIP-std+/-MMD and CIRMweigh-std+/-MMD (whose penalty targets the correct moment) recover the advantage. This is structurally the same "wrong statistic targets the wrong subspace of the perturbation ⟹ power/benefit vanishes exactly, not gracefully" pattern already catalogued for CI-test-statistic vs. perturbation-covariance mismatch (1501.01332 / 1706.08576), but demonstrated here for **regularization-penalty type vs. intervention type** in a constrained-optimization DA estimator — a new instantiation, same underlying shape.
- **Known rough causal structure is a hard requirement for mixed graphs, general case left open**: to handle graphs where some covariates are causal parents of Y and others are effects, the paper requires knowing *which* covariates are which (X_parent vs. X_descendant) to construct DIP⋄/CIP⋄/CIRM⋄ via a two-step residualization. The fully general mixed-causal-anticausal DA problem *without* this structural knowledge is explicitly flagged as open ("We leave the most general mixed-causal-anticausal domain adaptation... to future work").
- **Real-data check (Amazon Review, d=482, 15 environments)**: without known causal structure or intervention type, DIPweigh/CIP/CIRMweigh beat SrcPool in only 1 of 15 held-out target environments; revealing 20/40/60 target labels to *pick* the best method among {SrcPool, DIPweigh, CIP, CIRMweigh} recovers a method that is never worse than SrcPool and sometimes better. Cites a concurrent large-scale empirical study (Wiles et al. 2021, arXiv:2110.11328, appears as a forward citation of this paper) reaching the same "no single best DA method" conclusion across many real/synthetic shifts.

### 核心结果

| Setting | Winner vs baseline | Margin |
|---|---|---|
| Anticausal, single source, no Y-interv (Sim i) | DIP beats OLSSrc | 97–98/100 |
| Anticausal, multi-source, no Y-interv (Sim ii) | DIPweigh(M=8) beats DIP(1) | 89/100 |
| Anticausal, multi-source, Y-interv, **has CICs** (Sim iii) | CIRMweigh beats SrcPool | ~95–100/100 (≈5% rare losses) |
| Anticausal, multi-source, Y-interv, **no CICs** (Sim vi) | CIRMweigh beats SrcPool | **64/100 — near chance** |
| **Causal**, single source (Sim iv) | DIP **loses to** OLSSrc | 100/100 (always worse) |
| **Mixed**, single source (Sim v) | DIP loses to OLSSrc; DIP⋄ (oracle roles) beats OLSSrc | consistent |
| **Mixed**, multi-source, Y-interv, has CICs (Sim vii) | CIRMweigh beats SrcPool only 64/100; oracle CIRM⋄weigh beats SrcPool 96/100 | assumption gap ≈ 32pp |
| Variance-shift interv., mean-matching penalty (Sim viii) | DIP-mean ties OLSSrc exactly (0 benefit); DIP-std+/MMD wins | penalty-type match required |
| Real data, Amazon Review, 15 envs | Advanced DA beats SrcPool in 1/15 envs only | matches Wiles et al. 2021 |

### 明确局限

- Purely **i.i.d. cross-sectional multi-environment** setting (image batches, product-category text, synthetic linear SCM draws) — **no dynamical system, no time series, no autoregressive/lagged structure anywhere in the paper**. Nothing here is a direct time-series analogue of the fragility-regime question; it is an analogy from a neighboring (non-temporal) invariance-learning literature, not a temporal instantiation.
- Theory is for **linear** SCMs with **noise interventions only** (mean-shift or variance-shift); nonlinear SCMs and interventions that change the structural matrix B itself are explicitly out of scope ("theoretical extension to nonlinear SCMs is a challenging future direction").
- General mixed-causal-anticausal DA *without* known parent/descendant roles is an acknowledged open problem, not solved here.
- CIP/CIRM guarantees require knowing (or correctly estimating) M, and the "no-CICs" regime (Sim vi/vii) shows the paper's own new method (CIRM) degrading to near-baseline performance — the authors do not claim CIRM is robust to this violation, they just report it honestly.
- Single-group authorship for the theory; no adversarial/independent re-derivation within the paper itself (though heavily cited/extended since, e.g. by Wu, Chen & Ha 2309.10301 with an overlapping author).

### 跟我们的关系

No direct novelty collision with 0710-causal-scs: this paper's estimator family (DIP/CIP/CIRM, linear multi-environment regression DA) is a *different sub-literature* from PCMCI+ time-series causal discovery and from the graph-fragility-indicator framing of the idea — no shared method, no shared experimental setting, no shared claim to check for overlap.

Where it **is** useful, as analogical/corroborating evidence rather than a competing claim:
1. **Independently corroborates mechanism #3's boundary** (environments vs. dimension) in a second, non-IRM estimator family, with an explicit non-asymptotic constant (d ≥ 6M) — if the causal-scs-indicator idea needs to argue "the environments≤dimension collapse is a general phenomenon, not an IRM-specific quirk," this paper is a second independent proof to cite.
2. **Sharpens the boundary of mechanism #1** (CI-test power decay with conditioning-set size): explicitly distinguishes "more environments used for averaging" (bias↓, no power concept) from "more conditioning variables in a single test" (power↓). Worth updating the mechanism catalog's phrasing so it isn't accidentally applied to environment-count axes where the opposite trend holds.
3. **New estimator-family-agnostic data point for "assumption violation ⟹ collapse toward chance"**: the 64/100 (near-coin-flip) vs. 96–100/100 (near-oracle) contrast between "no CICs" and "has CICs" regimes is a clean quantitative example directly analogous to "PCMCI+ falls to chance in the fragility regime" — different method family, same qualitative shape. Useful as a supporting citation for the generality of that claim, though it carries zero temporal/dynamical-systems evidence.
4. **Two transferable factorial design templates**: (a) the 9-cell {source-count × causal-direction × intervention-type × Y-intervention × CIC-existence} sweep, evaluated via 100-repeat win-rate/scatterplot-diagonal-count rather than a single risk number — directly reusable as a regime-scan protocol for a fragility-indicator study; (b) the partial-label/partial-oracle informativeness sweep (reveal 20/40/60 target labels to see how much side information is needed to pick the correct winning method) — a genuinely new template dimension (scanning *how much partial ground truth is needed to resolve method choice*) not present in the previously catalogued 1707.06422 (γ×N) or 2010.05761 (env/dim × direction) templates.
5. Flags **2309.10301** (Wu, Chen, Ha — direct theoretical follow-up on conditionally invariant components, overlapping author with this paper) and **2502.02710** (Kostin, Gnecco, Yang — partial identification of robust risk) as high-priority next reads: both look like they could contain a more refined/rigorous version of exactly the "boundary condition under assumption violation" question this round is asking about.

## Read by: 0710-causal-scs (2026-07-11)

**撞车评估**: 无直接撞车。本文属于线性 SCM 下的多环境回归 domain adaptation 文献（DIP/CIP/CIRM 估计量族），与 PCMCI+ 时序因果发现 + fragility-indicator 的具体方法和实验设定完全不同子领域，没有可比较的具体 claim。

**核心增量（本轮非纯重复确认）**:
1. 用一个独立于 IRM 的估计量族（CIP/CIRM，闭式约束最小二乘而非优化 landscape）重新证出机制③"环境数 vs 维度"边界，并给出显式非渐近样本复杂度常数 d ≥ 6M——为该边界条件的"estimator-family-agnostic"属性提供第二个独立证据。
2. 概念澄清，收窄机制①的适用范围：CIP/IRM 中"更多环境"是均值化/估计资源（更多环境→bias 更小，无功效衰减概念），而 PCMCI+/ICP 式"在单次检验中增加条件变量"是功效衰减机制——两者不是同一个轴，机制①的表述需要限定在"单次检验条件集增大"场景，不能推广到"环境数增大"场景。
3. 新增一个 estimator-family-agnostic 的"假设违反→坍缩至近 chance"量化实例：CIC 存在时 CIRMweigh 胜率 95–100/100，CIC 不存在时坍缩到 64/100（接近硬币翻转），且 Example 4 中 CIP 与 SrcPool 完全打平（0.100 = 0.100，零增益而非渐进退化）。这与本课题"PCMCI+ 在 fragility regime 判别力落 chance"的现象在不同方法族（线性 DA 而非 CI 检验/anchor regression/IRM）中独立复现，加强了该现象的普遍性论据。
4. 机制④（检验统计量/扰动矩错配）新实例：DA 匹配惩罚类型（mean/std/MMD）与真实扰动类型（mean-shift/variance-shift）不匹配时，方法精确退化为零增益（而非部分增益）——与 1501.01332/1706.08576 的 CI 检验统计量错配机制同构，但是在正则化惩罚这一新载体上验证。
5. 两个可直接迁移的受控 factorial 设计模板：(a) {源环境数×因果方向×扰动类型×Y是否被扰动×CIC是否存在} 9 格设计，用 100 次重复的 win-rate/散点图对角线计数作为指标而非单一风险数值；(b) 部分标签/部分 oracle 信息量扫描（揭示 20/40/60 个目标标签以挑选最优方法）——扫描"需要多少部分真实信息才能分辨哪个方法赢"，是先前模板（1707.06422 的 γ×N、2010.05761 的 env/dim×方向）都没有的新维度。
