# HRRRCast: a data-driven emulator for regional weather forecasting at convection allowing scales (2507.05658)

- **Year**: 2025.07
- **Authors**: Daniel Abdi, Isidora Jankov, Paul Madden, Vanderlei Vargas, Timothy A. Smith, Sergey Frolov, Montgomery L. Flora, Corey K. Potvin — NOAA GSL/CIRES/CIRA/PSL/CIWRO/NSSL
- **Venue**: arXiv preprint (`\journal{-}` unset in source — appears not yet formally accepted at a journal as of this cached version)
- **Keywords**: HRRR emulator, convection-allowing model, SE-ResNet, GraphCast/GNN, FiLM, DDIM diffusion, ensemble forecasting, regional LAM
- **One-liner**: NOAA builds a 6km-CONUS ResNet+diffusion (ResHRRR) and GNN (GraphHRRR) emulator of HRRR, beating HRRR at light-rain FSS and matching it at moderate thresholds.

## 精华提炼

### 管线/方法

**ResHRRR (primary architecture, ~23.5M params)** — confirmed via Figure 1 (`pics/resnet.png`) plus §3.2.1:
- Inputs concatenated into one tensor before the encoder: HRRR state at time `t` (74 ch, weighted `1-m`) + noised HRRR / target-side forcing at `t+L` (74 ch, weighted trainable `m`) + GFS conditioning (28 ch) + static fields (orography height, land mask; 2 ch). Text caption states the encoder ingests **180 channels** at **530×900** spatial resolution; my own channel sum from the four named blocks is 178 — a ~2-channel reconciliation gap the paper does not resolve (possibly the 2 time-embedding scalars are *also* broadcast as extra input-map channels in addition to being fed into FiLM, but this is not stated explicitly).
- Two trainable skip connections (learnable scalar weights `1-m`, `m`, via `⊗`/`⊕` in the figure) blend the clean HRRR input and its noised counterpart, added directly to the residual tower's output — inspired by Karras et al. 2022 (EDM), gives the network a diffusion-step-dependent way to weight "trust the input" vs "trust the denoised state."
- 2× encoder blocks (SE-ResNet units + max-pool) downsample 530×900×180 → latent **130×225×192**.
- 26× processor blocks (SE-ResNet, same spatial dims throughout) — GraphCast-style "processor" terminology carried over even though this is a CNN, not a GNN, at this stage.
- 2× decoder blocks (SE-ResNet + upsample + U-Net encoder-skip concatenation) → back to 530×900×**74** output channels (HRRR-state-only; GFS/static channels are inputs only, never predicted).
- All SE-ResNet blocks use **LayerNorm**, not BatchNorm — explicitly chosen for stability under small batch size + time-dependent (FiLM) conditioning.
- FiLM block: dense layers turn (lead-time, diffusion-step) embeddings into per-channel `(γ, β)` that modulate encoder, processor, decoder, and the trainable skip connections — i.e., time-conditioning is injected at *every* stage, not just once.
- Diffusion: DDPM forward process with **T=200** steps, cosine `β`-schedule (`s=0.008` offset); inference uses **DDIM**, 30–50 sampling steps, deterministic (`σ=0`) or lightly stochastic.
- Multi-lead-time training: a *single* ResHRRR is trained jointly on **1h/3h/6h** targets (uniformly sampled per batch, lead-time fed via FiLM); longer horizons at inference use a **greedy autoregressive rollout** (e.g., 38h = six 6h steps + one shorter step).
- Loss: pressure-weighted MSE (GraphCast-style weighting, per-variable level-weights sum to 1), REFC weight 1.0, T2M weight 0.1, latitude weighting applied but "minimal effect" away from poles (LAM domain).
- **Missing from the paper entirely**: optimizer choice, learning rate, batch size, epoch count, and GPU/hardware budget are never stated in the main text (checked all of §3 Training). This is a real reporting gap, not something I could not find — I read the full Training section and it jumps straight from loss definition to multi-lead-time strategy.

**GraphHRRR** — GNN variant, ~37M params (larger latent, larger param count than ResHRRR):
- Directly adapted from GraphCast (Lam et al. 2023) via the same-author-group WoFSCast adaptation (Flora & Potvin 2025) — the paper explicitly says "we refer readers to these works" rather than re-deriving the GNN mechanics.
- Three stated architecture changes vs GraphCast: (a) Delaunay triangulation mesh replaces the icosahedral global mesh (fits a rectangular LAM domain); (b) Dirichlet boundary conditions (initialized from a global model) replace periodic/cyclic boundaries; (c) HRRR's native Lambert Conformal Conic (LCC) grid replaces lat-lon.
- **Explicitly immature**: current GraphHRRR is deterministic-only (no DDIM), does **not** ingest GFS synoptic input at all, and is trained only for 1h lead time then rolled out 6× for a 6h forecast — which the paper says "compound[s] the degradation in forecast quality." Authors call it a groundwork/future-direction component, not production-ready.
- In the one place GraphHRRR wins (power-spectrum high-frequency retention vs deterministic ResHRRR), the authors attribute it ambiguously to either the larger latent/param count or input-normalization differences — not a controlled ablation.

### 关键技术细节

- **Training data**: 3 years HRRR analysis + GFS, March 2021–March 2024 train; two eval sets — 4-month (Apr–Jul 2024, 6-hourly init, ~488 timestamps, forecasts to 48h) and a 10-day high-impact window (May 1–10 2024, hourly init, 240 timestamps, forecasts to 18h, covers the May 6–10 2024 tornado outbreak).
- **Ground truth = HRRR analysis**, not forecast (unlike StormCast, which the authors say *inadvertently* trained on +1h forecast data) — chosen because HRRR reanalysis is not publicly available and analysis is "the highest-quality HRRR dataset available."
- **Resolution**: full CONUS at **6 km**, grid 530×900 — an explicit, hardware-driven subsample (every other point) of the native 3 km, 1059×1799 HRRR grid. The paper is unambiguous that this is a resolution *trade-off* for full-domain coverage, not merely a display/eval choice — the network's spatial conv stem is literally sized for 530×900.
- **Pressure levels, HRRR side**: 12 levels — 200, 300, 475, 800, 825, 850, 875, 900, 925, 950, 975, 1000 hPa (6 atmospheric vars: TMP, HGT, UGRD, VGRD, WGRD, SPFH) + surface REFC + T2M = 74 ch.
- **Pressure levels, GFS side**: 4 levels — 200, 500, 850, 1000 hPa (same 6 vars) + REFC + MSLET/PRMSL + PRES ≈ 27 ch (paper states "28" as the intended design but discloses GFS T2M was omitted "due to an oversight," so actual training likely used 27).
- **Verification**: grid-based (POD/SR/CSI/FB via 2×2 contingency), neighborhood-based (Fractions Skill Score, 6/30/54/90 km pooling), and object-based (MODE tool within NCAR/DTC's MET framework) — three independent metric families, consistent conclusions across all three.
- **Params**: ResHRRR ≈23.5M, GraphHRRR ≈37M.
- **Ensemble**: 3–10 members generated by perturbing only the initial Gaussian noise (found sufficient for spread); Probability-Matched Mean (Potvin et al. 2019 method) used for reflectivity ensemble-mean reconstruction.

### 核心结果

| Metric | Finding |
|---|---|
| FSS @20dBZ, 6km pooling | HRRRCast beats HRRR at **all** lead times to 48h, even with only 3 members |
| FSS @30dBZ | HRRRCast beats HRRR to ~7h lead; HRRR regains advantage beyond that |
| FSS @40dBZ | HRRR slightly better throughout — neither model reaches "usable skill" (FSS≥0.4) at this threshold |
| Frequency Bias | HRRR over-forecasts (FB 1.2–2.7); HRRRCast under-forecasts (FB 0.4–0.8) — opposite failure modes |
| RMSE | Deterministic HRRRCast has *lower* RMSE than even the 10-member ensemble on most vars, but *worse* FSS at high dBZ — classic smoothing/blur tradeoff (RMSE rewards blur, FSS penalizes it) |
| Spread-error ratio | ~0.6 on average — ensemble is **under-dispersive** (honestly disclosed in Appendix A, not hidden) |
| Power spectra | Diffusion-based ResHRRR (HRRRCast) preserves high-frequency structure far better than either deterministic model; GraphHRRR retains slightly more fine-scale content than deterministic ResHRRR despite worse overall skill |
| Object-based (MODE) | HRRRCast ensemble beats deterministic HRRR forecast on POD/CSI/FB/SR for REFC>20dBZ |

**Surprising/notable**: the paper is unusually candid that HRRR's known failure mode (over-forecasting reflectivity) and HRRRCast's failure mode (under-forecasting) are *opposite*, and explicitly declines to declare a universal winner — frames the choice as operationally dependent on whether missed events or false alarms cost more.

### 明确局限

- **6km, not 3km** — self-disclosed as a hardware-driven compromise, most consequential fact for any downstream reuse at native HRRR resolution.
- GraphHRRR is explicitly unfinished (no diffusion, no GFS input, no multi-lead training) — the paper does not recommend it as a strong standalone model in its current form.
- No formal ablation isolating which architectural choice (REFC in GFS inputs vs multi-lead training vs analysis-vs-forecast targets) drives which performance gain — the paper says this directly ("we have not yet conducted a formal ablation study").
- No optimizer/LR/batch-size/epoch/compute-budget reporting anywhere in the main text.
- No code/data/weight availability statement anywhere in the arXiv source I read (verified by full-text read + `grep -niE "github|zenodo|huggingface|data availability|code availab|checkpoint|repository"` on the cached `main.tex` — zero matches). See Availability section below for what I found *outside* the paper itself.
- 40dBZ regime: neither model has "usable skill" — this is the operationally important high-end-severity regime and it's the one where the emulator is weakest relative to physics-based HRRR.

### 与本任务(HRRR 3km / 4气压层 / 96h历史 / storm-level分类)的匹配度

Checked against the four things the dispatcher asked me to judge specifically:

1. **Resolution**: mismatch. ResHRRR's conv stem is fixed at 530×900 (6km); the proposal's raw branch needs native 3km (1059×1799 or a storm-relative crop thereof). Feeding 3km data through this encoder requires re-gridding down to 6km first, which throws away exactly the fine-scale structure the proposal's Gate-0/P2 design is trying to test increments on top of.
2. **Pressure levels**: partial mismatch, not a clean subset. HRRRCast's HRRR-side input schema is a fixed 12-level, 74-channel (or 141-channel in the live-repo variant) tensor. The proposal's 4 levels (1000/850/500/250 hPa) hit 1000 and 850 exactly but **neither 500 nor 250 hPa exists in HRRRCast's level list** (closest are 475 and 300/200 hPa respectively) — there is no clean "just take 4 of the 12 channels" slicing available at the *input* side, because a frozen first-conv-layer's weights are tied to the specific 12-level ordering it was trained on.
3. **96h history**: this is the most fundamental mismatch, more severe than resolution or levels. ResHRRR is architecturally a **single-timestep-in, single-timestep-out** state emulator (input = HRRR at time `t` + GFS forcing at `t+L` → output = HRRR at `t+L`); the Figure 1 diagram has no temporal-history axis at all. It is not a history encoder in any sense the proposal needs. "Freeze the encoder, add a shallow probe head" would in practice mean: run the frozen encoder 96 times (once per historical hour) → get 96 separate 130×225×192 per-timestep latents → then still need a **new, from-scratch-trained temporal-aggregation module** (RNN/Transformer/temporal-conv) to fuse those 96 latents before any probe head can see the full 96h window. That aggregation module is itself a new spatiotemporal backbone component — which is exactly what the proposal's Non-goals section rules out ("不构建新的时空骨干网络"). This reframes the reviewers' "swap in a frozen encoder" suggestion as something closer to "add a new trainable temporal-fusion stage on top of a frozen spatial feature extractor," a materially bigger and more novel intervention than a shallow probe head.
4. **Task type**: HRRRCast is pretrained via regression (pixel-wise MSE state reconstruction), not classification. Regression→classification probing is a standard and generally reasonable transfer pattern in principle (compare Rasp & Thuerey 2021's own precedent of pretrain-on-climate-sim → fine-tune-on-reanalysis, itself regression→regression), but I found no literature precedent specifically for "freeze a regional NWP emulator's spatial encoder + probe head for storm-level severe-weather event classification" — my search for this pattern (`arxiv_tool.py search "frozen pretrained weather foundation model encoder probe severe weather classification downstream"`) hit S2/OpenAlex rate-limiting and fell back to arXiv's own keyword search, which returned only irrelevant heliophysics/space-weather papers (a known ambiguity of the word "weather"). I could not find supporting *or* contradicting precedent — this specific transfer pattern looks like a genuinely open, unexplored step, not a validated recipe the proposal could cite as de-risked.

**Bottom line for the P2 raw-only-branch decision**: the "same author group, real HRRR emulator" premise in the reviewer feedback is factually correct (Flora & Potvin are literally co-authors on both this paper and the already-cited WoFSCast paper). But the specific mechanism suggested — freeze ResHRRR/GraphHRRR's encoder, attach a shallow probe head — runs into three independent, non-cosmetic mismatches (resolution, pressure levels, and most importantly the complete absence of a temporal-history dimension in the architecture). Adopting it as-is would require enough new machinery (regridding, level-interpolation, and especially a new temporal-fusion module) that it risks quietly reintroducing the "new backbone" scope the proposal explicitly disclaims, and — because the exact weights/checkpoint availability is still unconfirmed — carries real execution risk on top of the conceptual mismatch. The proposal's own already-drafted fallback (lightweight masked-reconstruction/next-step self-supervised pretraining on its *own* training-fold HRRR windows, at its *own* native 3km/4-level/96h shape, then freeze) sidesteps all three mismatches simultaneously and does not depend on an external repo's uncertain weight availability. I present this as a factual tension for the next proposal-refiner iteration to weigh, not as a decision I'm making for them.
