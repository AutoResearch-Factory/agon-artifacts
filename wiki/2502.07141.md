# Small steps no more: Global convergence of stochastic gradient bandits for arbitrary learning rates (2502.07141)

- **Year**: 2025.02 (NeurIPS 2024)
- **Authors**: Jincheng Mei, Bo Dai, Alekh Agarwal, Sharan Vaswani, Anant Raj, Csaba Szepesvári, Dale Schuurmans (Google DeepMind, Google Research, Georgia Tech, SFU, IISc, U Alberta)
- **Venue**: NeurIPS 2024
- **Keywords**: stochastic gradient bandit, policy gradient, global convergence, arbitrary learning rate, exploration-exploitation, softmax parameterization, almost sure convergence
- **One-liner**: Proves that the stochastic gradient bandit algorithm converges to a globally optimal policy almost surely with **any** constant learning rate η > 0 — no small learning rate needed, no decay schedule needed.

## 精华提炼

### 管线/方法
**Setting**: Stochastic K-arm bandit with true mean reward vector r ∈ ℝᴷ, reward bounded in [-R_max, R_max]. Policy π_θ = softmax(θ). Goal: max_θ π_θᵀr.

**Algorithm**: Stochastic Gradient Bandit (Algorithm 1) — each iteration:
1. Sample action a_t ~ π_θt(·)
2. Observe reward R_t(a_t) ~ P_{a_t}
3. For chosen arm a_t: θ_{t+1}(a_t) ← θ_t(a_t) + η·(1-π_θt(a_t))·R_t(a_t)
4. For all other arms a ≠ a_t: θ_{t+1}(a) ← θ_t(a) - η·π_θt(a)·R_t(a_t)
5. Equivalent to: θ_{t+1} ← θ_t + η·(diag(π_θt) - π_θt π_θt^⊤)·r̂_t (unbiased IS gradient estimate)

**Core proof pipeline** (novel, not relying on smoothness/noise-control):
1. Define N_∞(a) = lim_{t→∞} N_t(a): asymptotic sample count per arm
2. Extended Borel-Cantelli Lemma bridges N_∞(a) = ∞ ⟺ Σ π_θt(a) = ∞
3. Lemma 1 (finite sample → bounded param): If N_∞(a) < ∞ → sup|θ_t(a)| < ∞
4. Lemma 2 (at least 2 arms sampled ∞ often): |A_∞| ≥ 2 almost surely — avoids exploration collapse
5. Progress-noise decomposition: θ_t(a) = E[θ_1(a)] + ΣW_s(a) + ΣP_s(a), where P_t = progress term, W_t = martingale noise. Freedman's inequality bounds noise; progress dominates asymptotically
6. Theorem 1 (K=2): Both arms sampled ∞ often → sub-martingale/super-martingale arguments → θ_t(a*)→∞, θ_t(subopt)→-∞ → π_θt(a*)→1
7. Theorem 2 (K≥2): Contradiction-based two-case proof:
   - Case 1: If N_∞(a*) < ∞ → leads to contradiction via lem:unbounded_prob_ratio
   - Case 2: If N_∞(a*) = ∞ → recursive argument eliminates suboptimal arms one by one in descending reward order
8. Theorem 3 (rate): O(ln T / T) on averaged iterates, via difference inequality bounding

### 关键技术细节
- **Parameterization**: Softmax over K actions, θ ∈ ℝᴷ (unbounded)
- **Learning rate**: ANY η ∈ Θ(1) (constant), e.g., η = 1, 10, 100, 1000 all converge
- **Reward model**: R_t(a) bounded in [-R_max, R_max], arbitrary distribution P_a with mean r(a)
- **Assumption**: No ties in true mean rewards (Assumption 1); authors conjecture it's removable
- **Key lemma not in prior work**: Extended Borel-Cantelli (Corollary 5.29, Breiman 1992) — connects event frequency to sum of conditional probabilities
- **Noise concentration**: Freedman's inequality (1975) — martingale concentration with variance-dependent bounds
- **Key technical trick**: Progress-noise decomposition — θ decomposes into cumulative progress (deterministic direction) + cumulative noise (martingale). Since noise appears under √ while progress is linear, progress always dominates asymptotically regardless of η
- **Variance proxy**: V_t(a) = (5/18)·Σ π_s(a)·(1-π_s(a)) — dominates conditional variance of noise
- **Simulation setup**: K=4, r=(0.2, 0.05, -0.1, -0.4), Gaussian rewards σ=0.1, 10⁶ iterations, 10 seeds per η
- **Prior bound for constant η**: η_c ≈ 0.00007 (Mei 2024, Lemma 4.6) — this paper uses η up to 1000 (10⁷× larger)

### 核心结果
| Result | Statement | Technique |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Lemma 2 | At least 2 arms sampled ∞ often, almost surely | Pigeonhole + contradiction via Borel-Cantelli |
| Theorem 1 (K=2) | π_θt(a*) → 1 a.s. for any constant η | Sub/super-martingale + progress-noise dominance |
| Theorem 2 (K≥2) | π_θt(a*) → 1 a.s. for any constant η | Contradiction + recursive arm elimination |
| Theorem 3 (rate) | O(ln T / T) average suboptimality | Difference inequality + Freedman concentration |
| Empirical | η=1,10 converge steadily; η=100,1000 converge faster in final stage but some seeds "stuck" | 4-arm Gaussian bandit, 10⁶ steps |

**Surprising findings**:
1. Learning rate is essentially irrelevant for asymptotic convergence — completely contradicts classical optimization theory where η must be ≤ 1/L (smoothness constant)
2. Larger η leads to FASTER convergence in the final stage (e−200 precision with η=1000 vs e−14 with η=1)
3. But larger η = higher risk of long plateaus near suboptimal corners (3/10 seeds stuck at 10⁶ steps for η=1000)
4. The algorithm automatically balances exploration-exploitation without explicit exploration bonus — the softmax Jacobian (diag(π) - ππ^⊤) provides inherent exploratory behavior
5. Non-monotone objective improvement is the norm with larger η — unlike small-η regime where expected reward improves monotonically

### 明确局限
1. **作者自述**: 仅限于 tabular softmax bandit，不扩展到 general RL 或 function approximation
2. **作者自述**: 没有刻画不同 η 对收敛行为的具体影响 (只有 asymptotic，没有 finite-time characterization)
3. **作者自述**: 依赖无 tie 假设 (Assumption 1)，虽然作者 conjecture 可以去掉
4. **我们读出的问题**: 收敛率 O(ln T/T) 是 averaged iterate 而非 last iterate，且常数中含有 η 和 R_max, K 的复杂依赖，实际指导意义有限
5. **我们读出的问题**: 实验过于简单 (synthetic 4-arm Gaussian, 10 seeds)，不构成有意义的 empirical validation
6. **没有 regret analysis**: 只证明了 asymptotic convergence，没有 regret bound，无法与 UCB/TS 等标准方法在 sample efficiency 上对比
7. **没有 finite-time guarantee**: 所有结果都是 asymptotic (t→∞)，finite-time convergence.tex 被注释掉了，仅有 9 行的 outline
8. **RL 扩展完全是 future work**: 没有提供任何从 bandit 到 MDP 的路径
9. **跟 0616-fex 关系**: 理论方法论上有参考价值 (progress-noise decomposition, Borel-Cantelli for exploration)，但该论文解决的问题 (bandit learning rate robustness) 与 FEX (feature explanation / feature extraction 相关课题) 可能不直接撞车
